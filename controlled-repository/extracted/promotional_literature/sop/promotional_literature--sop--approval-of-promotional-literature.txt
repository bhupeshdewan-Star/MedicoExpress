PURPOSE
This procedure describes the process for approval of promotional literature of medicinal products prepared by Product Management Team.
SCOPE
This SOP is applicable to all the designated personnel responsible for approval of promotional literature of medicinal products.
RESPONSIBILITY
The designated personnel reviewing promotional literature of medicinal product is responsible for scrutinizing it and discusses it with the Medical Director for review, comments and final approval before dispatching it to the concerned person.
POLICY
The first copy of all SOPs will be maintained in a soft copy form with the original signatures in the custody of the Quality Assurance Department. Second copy will be made available in the controlled form for reference by employees. The forms, templates and checklists will have restricted access to employees.
GENERAL PRINCIPLES
The approval of the promotional literature should be done using following guiding principles:
Approvals should be done on a first come first serve basis and preferably on the same day when they are received.
PMT team should be asked to prepare pre-artwork promotional literature in the form of Power Point presentations.
Corrections if any should be intimated on same day with proper comments mentioned in the Power Point presentations.
Corrections should be easy to locate, preferably highlighted.
The corrections suggested in a promotional literature should be supported with valid scientific information.
PROCEDURES
Checking the scientific basis of promotional claims:
Promotional information should be clear, legible, accurate, balanced, fair, objective and sufficiently complete to enable the recipient to form his or her own opinion of the therapeutic value of the pharmaceutical product concerned.
Promotional information, Pictures, Graphs, Charts provided in the literature should be relevant to the medicinal product concerned.
Promotional information, Pictures, Graphs, Charts provided in the literature should be supported by Scientific/Medical references.
Scientific/Medical references provided in the literature should be clear, authentic and easily retrievable.
Promotional information should be based on an up-to-date evaluation of all relevant evidence.
Absolute or all-embracing claims should be used with caution and only with adequate qualification and substantiation.
It should not mislead by distortion, exaggeration, undue emphasis, omission or in any other way. Every effort should be made to avoid ambiguity.
Information in promotional materials must support proper assessment of the risks and benefits of the product and its appropriate use
Checking the adequacy of information:
Following information should be present in the promotional literature:
The name of the product (normally the brand name)
The active ingredients, using approved names where they exist.
The name and address of the pharmaceutical company or its agent responsible for marketing the product.
Date of production of the promotional literature.
Appropriate dose, route of administration, dosage form and dosing regimen for the mentioned indications should be adequately mentioned in the promotional literature.
“Abbreviated prescribing information”(ABPI) which should include an approved indication or indications for use together with dosage and method of use, and a succinct statement of the contraindications, precautions and side effects should be present in all Leave Behind Leaflets (LBL).
