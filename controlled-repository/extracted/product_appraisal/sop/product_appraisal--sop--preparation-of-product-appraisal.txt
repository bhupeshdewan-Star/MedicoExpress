Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) – Preparation of Product Appraisal
1. PURPOSE
Define the standardized process for preparing a comprehensive, evidence-based, decision-oriented Product Appraisal for medicinal products to support Go/No-Go decisions.
2. SCOPE
Applicable to all personnel involved in creation, review, approval, and updating of Product Appraisals.
3. RESPONSIBILITY
- Author drafts and updates appraisal.
- Medical Director reviews and approves.
- PMT uses for decision-making.
- QA maintains controlled copies.
- Cross-functional teams provide updated data.
4. POLICY
All appraisals must be evidence-based, referenced, updated, and aligned with regulatory and medico-marketing standards.
5. GENERAL PRINCIPLES
Appraisal must determine whether the product offers clinically meaningful, economically viable, and strategically differentiated value.
6. PROCEDURE
6.1 Title Page
Include document title, drug name, strength, route, classification, company details, dates.
6.2 Table of Contents
Include page numbers.
6.3 Summary Product Appraisal
Provide executive summary, key insights, Go/No-Go recommendation, place in therapy.
6.4 Introduction / Background
Describe problem statement, epidemiology, disease burden, management gaps, rationale.
6.5 Indian Epidemiology & Unmet Need Deep Dive
Include national/state epidemiology, DALYs, patient journey, access gaps, unmet needs.
6.6 Scientific Information
Pharmaceutical info, clinical pharmacology, PK/PD, indications, contraindications, dosage, interactions, warnings, ADRs.
6.7 Landmark Clinical Trials
Include pivotal efficacy/safety trials, comparative studies, BA/BE studies.
6.8 Competitive Landscape & Differentiation
Competitor analysis, differentiation matrix, pricing, market share, pipeline threats.
6.9 Health Economics & Cost-Effectiveness
CEA, CUA, BIA, affordability index, hospitalization cost impact.
6.10 Diagnostic & Stewardship Integration Strategy
Diagnostics required, stewardship alignment, algorithms, resistance impact.
6.11 Risk Management & Pharmacovigilance Plan
Identified/potential risks, missing info, RMP, PV system, PMS plan.
6.12 Medical Education & KOL Engagement Plan
KOL mapping, scientific narrative, advisory boards, CME, publications.
6.13 Regulatory Feasibility & Approval Pathway
DCGI pathway, required studies, timelines, risks, precedent approvals.
6.14 Manufacturing, CMC & Supply Chain Readiness
API source, CMC compliance, stability, scalability, COGS, supply chain risks.
6.15 Market Access & Pricing Strategy
Pricing corridor, reimbursement, tenders, formulary acceptance, PAPs.
6.16 Commercial Forecast & Scenario Planning
3-scenario forecast, assumptions, sensitivity, break-even, ROI.
6.17 Go/No-Go Decision Framework
Weighted scoring across clinical, regulatory, competitive, economic, operational, and risk domains.
6.18 Standard Guidelines Supporting New Drug
Include Indian and international guidelines.
6.19 International Approvals
USFDA, EMA, MHRA, PMDA, TGA status.
6.20 Patent Information
COM patents, process patents, expiry, FTO analysis.
6.21 SWOT Analysis
Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats.
6.22 Summary & Key Points
Unique pharmacology, efficacy, safety, clinical evidence, place in therapy, USPs.
6.23 References
Vancouver style, numbered citations.
