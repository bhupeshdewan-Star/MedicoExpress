# Pharmaceutical Product Training Slides Skill

Prepare a **high-standard, compliance-first slide deck specification** for a pharmaceutical medical department to create product training slides for medical representatives. The deck must align to the approved training manual, approved product monograph / prescribing information / SmPC / local label, and company medical-legal-regulatory standards. The output must support field-force training and guidance while remaining compliant with **UCPMP 2024** for India and **ABPI Code 2024** principles relevant to international / UK-standard promotional governance.[cite:1][cite:3]

## Purpose

Create a reusable, professional skill that guides generation of training slides for:
- product induction
- refresher training
- launch readiness
- objection handling
- FAQ handling
- query escalation
- compliance coaching
- role-play practice
- disease-state understanding
- scientific storytelling for detailing

The final slide set must help medical representatives explain the product accurately, fairly, and confidently **without making off-label, exaggerated, misleading, or inducement-linked claims**.[cite:1][cite:3]

## Core operating principles

1. Promote only within the approved label / marketing authorization / local prescribing information.[cite:1][cite:3]
2. All claims must be accurate, balanced, current, fair, objective, and capable of substantiation.[cite:1][cite:3]
3. Slides must distinguish clearly between:
   - approved promotional communication
   - non-promotional disease education
   - medical-information-only responses
   - adverse-event and product-quality complaint escalation.[cite:2][cite:3]
4. No gifts, inducements, improper hospitality, misleading superiority claims, disguised promotion, or public-POM promotion content.[cite:1][cite:3]
5. Representative behavior, wording, and visual material must reflect high professional standards and not cause confusion with editorial content.[cite:1][cite:3]
6. Every slide deck must be consistent with the training manual, product monograph, and current approved claims library.

## Inputs required

Before generating slides, gather and verify the following inputs:

### Product core
- brand name
- generic / INN name
- dosage form(s)
- strength(s)
- indication(s)
- approved patient population
- route of administration
- pack sizes
- legal classification / Rx status
- market(s) covered by the deck

### Medical source pack
- approved product monograph / SmPC / PI / package insert
- approved training manual
- approved claims compendium / master message platform
- approved visual aid / leave-behind source content
- approved FAQ / MI documents
- approved safety narrative
- approved competitor comparison source pack, if permitted

### Training deployment context
- country / region
- new launch vs lifecycle brand
- first-line / second-line / add-on / specialty / chronic / acute use
- target audience of reps: GP / specialist / hospital / retail / institutional
- expected duration: 10 / 20 / 30 / 45 / 60 minute workshop
- whether trainer notes are required
- whether assessment questions are required

### Compliance context
- whether deck is India-only, UK-standard, or international hybrid
- whether PI / prescribing information must appear as appendix or linked handout
- whether local pharmacovigilance statement, AE mailbox, and escalation SOP references are required

If any of these are missing, request them before drafting content.

## Deliverable structure

Generate a **slide-building specification** and then the **deck content plan**.

### Part A: Slide blueprint
For each slide, define:
- slide number
- slide title
- objective of slide
- audience takeaway
- key approved messages
- suggested visual type (diagram, table, patient journey, MoA schematic, anatomy panel, efficacy chart, safety matrix, FAQ card, objection-handling flow)
- speaker notes
- compliance guardrails
- references to source pack

### Part B: Deck content
Build the deck in the following order unless a shorter audience-adapted version is requested.

## Recommended slide architecture

### 1. Title slide
Include:
- product name
- generic name
- indication shorthand
- internal training label: “For internal training of field force only”
- version number
- approval / review date

### 2. Training goals
State what the MR should be able to do after training:
- explain disease-state basics
- identify the right patient type within label
- articulate mechanism and core value proposition
- discuss efficacy and safety appropriately
- handle routine HCP questions
- know what to escalate
- avoid non-compliant claims

### 3. Compliance opening
Create a concise but visible slide covering:
- on-label only
- fair balance
- no off-label discussion initiated by rep
- no unsupported comparisons
- no claims of absolute safety
- no inducement-linked behavior
- escalation of unsolicited medical queries to Medical Information / Medical Affairs
- adverse event reporting within required timelines.[cite:1][cite:3]

### 4. Product at a glance
Use a one-slide summary table:
- brand / generic
- class
- indication
- dosing headline
- differentiating approved attributes
- key contraindications / warnings headline
- key practical counseling points

### 5. Disease burden and unmet need
Explain only approved, referenced disease-state context.
Include:
- burden / prevalence if approved and current
- unmet need
- treatment goals
- where current management falls short
- why the product matters clinically

### 6. Human system fundamentals
Add foundational education so the rep understands the biology behind the disease and target.
Create simple teaching slides using diagrams/tables on:
- relevant anatomy
- relevant physiology
- pathophysiology
- site of action / organ system focus
- biomarker or pathway basics where relevant

Examples:
- cardiovascular: heart, vessels, plaque, thrombosis, hemodynamics
- diabetes/metabolic: pancreas, insulin axis, incretin system, liver, muscle, adipose
- respiratory: airway anatomy, inflammation, bronchoconstriction
- GI: acid pathway, gut motility, microbiome context if approved relevance exists
- CNS: neurotransmission basics, receptor targets, symptom circuitry

### 7. Patient type / right patient identification
Teach the MR to identify the **approved** patient profile only.
Cover:
- who is eligible
- typical patient archetype
- symptom / diagnostic / treatment-history clues
- when not appropriate
- red-flag exclusions
- referral / specialist escalation cues

### 8. Mechanism of action
Create a professional MoA module:
- what the drug binds / inhibits / activates
- downstream effect
- clinical relevance
- simple analogy for memory
- what not to overstate
- MoA to benefit bridge

### 9. Pharmacology / PK essentials
Only if relevant to field training.
Possible elements:
- onset / duration headline
- dosing frequency rationale
- food / timing relevance
- route and administration considerations
- renal / hepatic adjustment summary if approved
- interaction overview if clinically important and approved

### 10. Clinical evidence overview
Present evidence hierarchy clearly:
- pivotal trials
- supportive studies
- real-world evidence if approved for use
- endpoints
- patient population
- comparator
- duration
- major strengths / limitations

### 11–13. Efficacy slides
Use professional evidence presentation:
- primary endpoint
- key secondary endpoints
- subgroup insights only if approved and substantiated
- onset / magnitude / durability
- practical meaning for HCP
- avoid over-interpretation of numerically favorable but non-significant trends.[cite:3]

Include:
- clean graphs
- absolute numbers where possible
- denominators and timeline
- footnotes and references

### 14–15. Safety and tolerability
Cover:
- common adverse reactions
- serious / important risks
- contraindications
- warnings / precautions
- monitoring requirements
- special populations
- when rep must stop discussion and refer to PI / MI

Add a separate slide:
- “What reps must never say”
  - “completely safe”
  - “no side effects”
  - “best in class” unless approved factually and substantiated
  - any unsupported head-to-head superiority wording.[cite:1][cite:3]

### 16. Dosing and administration
Include:
- dose
- titration if any
- route
- administration steps
- missed dose basics if approved
- storage / handling if relevant
- demonstration device basics if applicable

### 17. Patient counseling essentials
Teach field teams what can be reinforced through HCP-facing training.
Include:
- adherence points
- administration tips
- what patient should expect
- common tolerability advice if approved
- warning signs for physician follow-up

### 18. Product positioning
Show where the product fits **within approved label and company strategy**:
- treatment line
- patient segment
- role versus current standard of care
- place in therapy statement
- message hierarchy: primary / secondary / support message

### 19. Competitor landscape
Create a compliant comparison framework.
Allowed approach:
- molecule / class overview
- dosage / route / frequency
- approved indication overlap
- evidence base level
- practical differentiators
- device / convenience attributes if factual

Rules:
- no disparagement
- no unfair imitation
- no cherry-picking
- no unsupported superiority language.[cite:1][cite:3]

### 20. Objection handling / query handling
Build a full field query-handling skill module.
Use a repeatable framework such as:
- **LACE** = Listen, Acknowledge, Clarify, Escalate/Explain
- **SAIL** = Stop, Assess, Inform with approved data, Log/Escalate
- **BRIDGE** = Bridge from question to approved evidence

For each common question, provide:
- likely HCP question
- intent behind the question
- approved short response
- approved supporting evidence
- what not to say
- whether to escalate to Medical

Cover question categories:
- efficacy magnitude
- comparative value
- safety concerns
- special populations
- dosing convenience
- cost / access
- evidence gaps
- off-label requests
- anecdotal-use questions
- competitor challenges

### 21. FAQs
Prepare a high-value FAQ bank with short and long answers.
Recommended categories:
- disease-state
- mechanism
- efficacy
- safety
- dose / administration
- interactions
- storage / handling
- practical use
- patient archetypes
- competitive questions
- medico-legal / compliance questions

Every FAQ should carry one of these response labels:
- Field-usable answer
- Field-usable with caution
- Medical-only escalation
- Pharmacovigilance escalation

### 22. Query escalation map
Create a decision-tree slide covering:
- unsolicited off-label question → refer to Medical Information
- adverse event → capture and report immediately
- product complaint / quality issue → quality escalation
- pregnancy / lactation query beyond label wording → medical escalation
- request for journal paper not in approved pack → medical escalation
- pricing / commercial contracting query → commercial team, no scientific improvisation

### 23. Do / Don’t slide
Build a memorable compliance slide.
Example:
- Do: use approved claims, present balance, document AE, carry PI, respect HCP time
- Don’t: speculate, exaggerate, compare unfairly, induce access, use unapproved visuals, share public-facing promotion for POMs.[cite:1][cite:3]

### 24. Acronyms and memory devices
Create rep-friendly memory tools. Examples:
- **RIGHT** patient = Right indication, In-label use, Guideline fit, History checked, Therapy match
- **CLEAR** call = Compliant, Label-based, Evidence-backed, Accurate, Respectful
- **SAFER** response = Stop, Assess, Fact-check, Escalate, Report
- **TRUST** detailing = Truthful, Relevant, Up-to-date, Substantiated, Transparent

Develop disease/product-specific acronyms too, for example:
- benefit pillars
- patient selection checklist
- safety recall
- administration sequence
- objection flow

### 25. Role-play module
Include structured scenarios:
- cooperative HCP
- skeptical HCP
- time-pressed HCP
- price-focused HCP
- safety-focused HCP
- competitor-loyal HCP
- off-label-seeking HCP

For each scenario provide:
- context
- desired rep behavior
- ideal opening line
- ideal close
- compliance trap to avoid
- trainer scoring rubric

### 26. Assessment / certification
Add questions in mixed format:
- MCQs
- true/false
- scenario-based
- “is this compliant?” mini-cases
- objection response drills

Score domains:
- science understanding
- product knowledge
- message discipline
- compliance discipline
- escalation judgment

### 27. Appendix
Include:
- abbreviations list
- glossary
- claims reference map
- source hierarchy
- version control
- medical / PV / quality contacts
- prescribing information / key monograph excerpts reference location

## Content standards

### Scientific standards
- Use approved source hierarchy: label / SmPC / PI first, then approved core data sheet, then approved publications.
- Separate pivotal evidence from exploratory analyses.
- Mention study limitations where needed for fair balance.
- Use absolute values and clinical context where possible.
- Avoid overstating surrogate endpoints.

### Compliance standards
- No off-label slide content except internal trainer-only escalation examples clearly marked as “do not proactively discuss”.[cite:1][cite:3]
- No claims of guaranteed efficacy, cure, zero-risk, or universal superiority.[cite:1][cite:3]
- Comparative slides require like-for-like factual comparison and references.[cite:1][cite:3]
- Prescribing-information and obligatory-information handling for promotional materials must follow the local market standard; ABPI 2024 specifically requires clear and legible prescribing information as part of promotional material, with options for QR code or direct single-click access in certain formats.[cite:3]
- Material must clearly identify company involvement and avoid disguised promotion.[cite:1][cite:3]

## Visual design guidance

Use a professional medical-department style:
- white / light neutral or dark navy scientific palette
- restrained color coding
- anatomy diagrams with clear labels
- pathway schematics with progressive arrows
- evidence tables with footnotes
- safety icons used sparingly
- competitor tables neutral in tone
- FAQs as accordion-style cards or matrix tables
- process diagrams for escalation routes

Preferred visuals by section:
- disease basics → anatomy / physiology infographic
- MoA → receptor / pathway schematic
- efficacy → bar chart / KM curve / endpoint table / responder matrix
- safety → adverse-event frequency table / precaution icons
- positioning → patient journey or treatment algorithm
- objection handling → conversational flowchart
- escalation → decision tree

## Speaker notes rules

Every slide must include presenter guidance with four subheads:
- **What to say**
- **What not to say**
- **If challenged**
- **If outside label or evidence**

## Query-handling library format

For each anticipated query, write it in this template:

### Query
[Exact HCP question]

### Approved response
[Concise field-usable answer]

### Expanded response
[Longer explanation for training use]

### Evidence anchor
[Label / study / approved source]

### Red line
[What rep must not say]

### Escalation?
[Yes/No + destination]

## Special sections to include

### A. Fundamentals mini-atlas
Prepare 3–5 foundational slides that simplify the organ system and disease focus for new reps.

### B. Common confusion pairs
Examples:
- efficacy vs effectiveness
- common AE vs serious risk
- class effect vs molecule-specific claim
- approved indication vs physician practice experience
- convenience claim vs outcome claim

### C. Myth vs fact
Build a compliant myth/fact section using only approved substantiated content.

### D. Field-ready one-minute pitch
Create:
- 15-second opener
- 30-second product summary
- 60-second value narrative
All must stay on-label and balanced.

### E. Manager coaching guide
Add a trainer appendix for FLMs / training managers:
- how to assess accuracy
- how to identify risky improvisation
- how to coach for brevity and balance
- how to score objection handling

## Output formats

## Approval checklist

Before finalizing, verify:
- aligned to training manual
- aligned to product monograph / PI / SmPC
- no off-label proactive messaging
- claims substantiated
- fair balance maintained
- safety and AE reporting included
- escalation pathways explicit
- comparator material factual and non-disparaging
- version control present
- internal-use-only statement present where needed
