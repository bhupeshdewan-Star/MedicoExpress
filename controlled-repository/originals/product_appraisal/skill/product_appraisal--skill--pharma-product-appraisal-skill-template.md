# Pharma Product Appraisal Skill Template

A structured appraisal framework for evaluating pharmaceutical products for in-licensing, acquisition, internal portfolio prioritization, new brand planning, lifecycle management, and strategic fit assessment. This template is designed to be detailed enough for analyst use and modular enough to adapt into a Codex-style reusable skill document.

## Purpose

This template helps teams evaluate a pharmaceutical product across scientific merit, development risk, regulatory position, intellectual property strength, pricing, market attractiveness, strategic fit, and execution readiness. Clinical trial transparency and completeness of evidence should be assessed explicitly because public availability of trial data and results can vary materially across companies and products.[cite:18][cite:20]

## How to Use

- Score each line item from 0 to 5.
- Use the scoring definitions below.
- Multiply the score by the assigned weight.
- Sum weighted scores to generate a total appraisal score out of 100.
- Apply mandatory red-flag rules before making a go/no-go decision.
- Use evidence triangulation across patents, trial registries, regulatory sources, publications, pricing checks, and structured web extraction because source completeness and transparency are uneven across the industry.[cite:18][cite:20]

## 1. Therapeutic Gap and Medical Need

Score the product on:

- Size of unmet need in the target indication.
- Disease burden, morbidity, mortality, and quality-of-life impact.
- Limitations of current standard of care.
- Gaps in efficacy, safety, adherence, tolerability, onset, durability, or convenience.
- Need in high-risk subgroups, refractory populations, pediatrics, geriatrics, or comorbid populations.
- Public-health relevance and epidemiologic momentum.
- Guideline gap that the molecule can address.
- Potential to alter treatment paradigm.

## 2. Molecule and Target Profile

Score the product on:

- Novelty of target or mechanism.
- Biological plausibility of target.
- Strength of target validation from human genetics, translational evidence, or class precedent.
- Differentiation versus molecules in the same class.
- Selectivity, potency, PK/PD rationale, and therapeutic index.
- Suitability of route, dose frequency, and formulation.
- Ease of positioning by mechanism for prescribers.
- Follow-on formulation opportunity, fixed-dose combination potential, and lifecycle expansion options.

## 3. Preclinical Evidence

Score the product on:

- Breadth and quality of pharmacology package.
- Translational validity of animal or in vitro models.
- PK/PD alignment to intended human dose.
- Toxicology completeness.
- Safety pharmacology status.
- Genotoxicity, reproductive toxicity, carcinogenicity readiness where relevant.
- Biomarker support and translational bridge.
- Manufacturability signal from early formulation and stability work.

## 4. Clinical Evidence

Score the product on:

- Development stage maturity.
- Strength of phase-appropriate evidence.
- Trial design quality, including randomization, blinding, comparator, powering, and endpoint selection.
- Clinical meaningfulness of effect size.
- Durability of efficacy.
- Quality of subgroup data.
- External validity and real-world relevance.
- Completeness of results disclosure.
- Publication quality and congress visibility.
- Trial transparency because results reporting and public availability can vary by company and product.[cite:18][cite:20]

## 5. Safety and Tolerability

Score the product on:

- Overall adverse-event burden.
- Serious adverse-event profile.
- Discontinuation and dose interruption rates.
- Class-related warnings and boxed-warning risk.
- Monitoring burden.
- Drug-drug interaction risk.
- Special population concerns.
- Immunogenicity, abuse potential, QT liability, hepatic or renal signal where relevant.
- Risk management feasibility.
- Expected physician comfort with the safety profile.

## 6. Regulatory Status and Pathway

Score the product on:

- Current regulatory status by geography.
- IND / CTA / NDA / BLA / MAA / ANDA / biosimilar pathway clarity.
- Likelihood of accelerated pathways, orphan designation, priority review, or breakthrough status.
- Approval history, CRLs, refusals, withdrawals, or major queries.
- Label breadth and restrictions.
- CMC readiness and filing-readiness signals.
- Country-specific registration complexity.
- Post-marketing commitment burden.
- Pharmacovigilance and risk-management obligations.

## 7. Patent and Exclusivity

Score the product on:

- Composition-of-matter patent strength.
- Secondary patent depth, including formulation, process, polymorph, salt, crystal, use, and device claims.
- Remaining exclusivity life.
- Patent landscape density.
- Freedom-to-operate risk.
- Litigation, opposition, revocation, or paragraph IV exposure.
- Data exclusivity, market exclusivity, orphan exclusivity, and pediatric extensions where relevant.
- White-space opportunities for lifecycle defense.

### Patent Search Checklist

- Core patent numbers and assignees.
- Earliest filing and expected expiry.
- Jurisdiction-wise coverage.
- Status of granted, pending, expired, opposed, or litigated assets.
- Patent family breadth.
- Blocking patents around formulation, process, delivery device, or indication.
- Generic / biosimilar challenge visibility.
- Freedom-to-operate summary and risk rating.

## 8. Competitor Landscape

Score the product on:

- Number and strength of approved competitors.
- Number and strength of pipeline competitors.
- Direct class competition and adjacent mechanism competition.
- Degree of efficacy differentiation.
- Degree of safety differentiation.
- Convenience differentiation, such as route, dosing, or administration burden.
- Time to crowding and expected launch window overlap.
- Guideline and KOL alignment.
- Switching barriers and entrenched physician habits.

## 9. Pricing and Market Access

Score the product on:

- Innovator price and expected net price logic.
- Competitor price positioning.
- Price corridor in the class.
- Reimbursement or payer acceptance potential.
- HTA attractiveness where relevant.
- Price elasticity and affordability.
- Current ceiling price or applicable MRP controls in regulated markets.
- Margin headroom after trade, institutional, and channel deductions.

## 10. API Economics and Supply

Score the product on:

- Current API cost trend.
- Number of qualified API suppliers.
- Dependence on China, Europe, India, or single-source vendors.
- DMF availability and quality.
- Cost volatility risk.
- Yield and impurity complexity.
- Logistics and lead-time risk.
- Backward integration potential.
- Exposure to solvent, intermediate, or starting-material bottlenecks.

## 11. Commercial Attractiveness

Score the product on:

- Market size and growth.
- Segment attractiveness.
- Prescriber concentration.
- Channel fit, such as retail, hospital, specialist, government, or institutional.
- Forecast volume potential.
- Gross-to-net profile.
- Franchise extension or combination opportunity.
- Promotional leverage and sales-force efficiency.

## 12. Strategic Fit with Company Product Mix

Score the product on:

- Fit with current therapy-area focus.
- Synergy with existing brands and field-force relationships.
- Overlap with current prescriber base.
- Cannibalization risk versus portfolio uplift.
- Fit with current distribution footprint.
- Fit with manufacturing capability and dosage-form know-how.
- Fit with company reputation and long-term strategic priorities.
- Contribution to portfolio balance by risk, lifecycle stage, and margin mix.

### Product Mix Fit Questions

- Does the molecule strengthen a core franchise or distract from it?
- Can the same HCP call point be leveraged efficiently?
- Does it complement existing combinations, adjunctive use, or line-extension strategy?
- Does the company have the regulatory, medical, and market-access muscle needed for this class?
- Does it improve portfolio defensibility or simply add complexity?

## 13. HCP Value Proposition

Score the product on:

- Why a physician should prescribe this product instead of current options.
- Clarity of efficacy advantage.
- Clarity of safety or tolerability advantage.
- Convenience advantage for initiation, maintenance, or monitoring.
- Fit with local treatment guidelines and real prescribing behavior.
- Ease of explaining the product in one concise clinical narrative.
- Expected trust based on evidence maturity.
- Relevance to specialist and GP prescribers where applicable.

### Why HCPs Should Prescribe It

Capture the prescribing argument in one-page form:

- Main patient problem solved.
- Most convincing efficacy claim.
- Most convincing safety or tolerability claim.
- Best convenience or adherence claim.
- Place in therapy versus current standard of care.
- Ideal patient profile.
- Top three objection-handling points.
- Evidence package needed for medical and promotional communication.

## 14. Operational and CMC Feasibility

Score the product on:

- Formulation complexity.
- Analytical-method maturity.
- Process robustness.
- Scale-up risk.
- Stability profile.
- Device dependency and secondary packaging complexity.
- Tech-transfer difficulty.
- Manufacturing capacity fit.
- Launch timeline realism.

## 15. Evidence Quality and Digital Intelligence

Score the product on:

- Source quality hierarchy, such as label, regulator, registry, peer-reviewed paper, conference abstract, patent, company deck, distributor site, scraped listing.
- Cross-source consistency.
- Reliability of scraped pricing or competitor information.
- Need for manual validation after web scraping.
- Breadth of evidence retrieved from patent search, trial databases, publications, and market sources.
- Gaps that materially affect decision quality.

### Digital Intelligence Inputs

- Patent search output.
- Clinical-trial registry extraction.
- Web-scraped competitor price table.
- Regulatory-status scraper or manually validated tracker.
- Publication mining and abstract capture.
- Pharmacovigilance signal review.
- KOL / guideline mapping.
- Distributor and tender price surveillance.

## 16. Reputation, Ethics, and Access

Score the product on:

- Public-health value.
- Equity and access considerations.
- Ethical concerns in promotion or positioning.
- Antimicrobial stewardship or abuse risk where relevant.
- Company reputation impact.

## Red Flags

A strong numeric score should not override the following issues if they are present:

- Inconsistent efficacy signal.
- Major unresolved safety risk.
- Patent cliff or blocking FTO issue.
- Regulatory pathway uncertainty with major endpoint risk.
- Unacceptable API cost or unstable supply.
- Ceiling-price restrictions that make margin unattractive.
- Poor fit with company product mix or call point.
- No compelling HCP prescribing rationale.
- Heavy market crowding with weak differentiation.
- Weak evidence integrity or overreliance on low-confidence scraped data.

## Final Decision Framework

| Total Score | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 85 to 100 | High-priority strategic asset | Pursue aggressively |
| 70 to 84 | Strong candidate | Shortlist and diligence deeply |
| 55 to 69 | Conditional candidate | De-risk key gaps first |
| Below 55 | Weak candidate | Deprioritize unless strategic exception exists |

## Suggested Skill Prompt for Codex

```text
Use this appraisal framework to evaluate a pharmaceutical product comprehensively. Extract and summarize molecule details, mechanism, indication, stage, patent landscape, exclusivity, clinical and preclinical evidence, regulatory status by geography, safety profile, competitor landscape, API price and sourcing risk, MRP ceiling or regulated price limits, competitor pricing, HCP prescribing rationale, therapeutic gap, commercial attractiveness, and strategic fit with the company’s current product mix. Build a weighted scorecard, flag critical red risks, and generate a one-page executive summary plus a detailed evidence table. ## Analyst Checklist
```

- Molecule and target understood.
- Therapeutic gap validated.
- Clinical data independently reviewed.
- Preclinical package checked.
- Regulatory status mapped by geography.
- Patent and exclusivity reviewed.
- Competitor data updated.
- API cost and supply checked.
- Ceiling price and MRP constraints reviewed.
- Competitor price benchmarked.
- HCP prescribing logic articulated.
- Strategic fit with company portfolio tested.
- CMC and launch feasibility checked.
- Source confidence rated.
- Final recommendation documented.
