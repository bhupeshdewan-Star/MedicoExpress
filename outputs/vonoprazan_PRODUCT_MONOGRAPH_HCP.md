# VOQUEZNA® (Vonoprazan Fumarate) - PROFESSIONAL MONOGRAPH

## Clinical Overview

**Trade Name:** VOQUEZNA®  
**Generic Name:** Vonoprazan fumarate  
**Drug Class:** Potassium-Competitive Acid Blocker (P-CAB)  
**FDA Approval:** February 28, 2024  
**Approved Indications:** Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD), Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD)  
**Dosage Strength:** 20 mg tablets  
**Recommended Dosage:** 20 mg once daily  

### Quick Clinical Summary

VOQUEZNA® is the first and only potassium-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB) approved by the FDA for the treatment of GERD and peptic ulcer disease. Unlike traditional proton pump inhibitors, vonoprazan provides rapid acid suppression (beginning within 30 minutes) with more complete intragastric pH elevation (>95% vs. ~90% for PPIs). It is particularly beneficial for patients with inadequate response to standard PPI therapy or those at risk for PPI-related adverse effects.

**Key Clinical Points:**
- ✅ First P-CAB approved in the United States
- ✅ Faster symptom relief vs. lansoprazole (complete symptom resolution by week 4)
- ✅ Superior H. pylori eradication rates (95% vs. 88% with PPI-based regimens)
- ✅ Better vitamin B12 preservation (2.4% vs. 8.7% with lansoprazole)
- ✅ Minimal drug-drug interaction potential (not CYP3A4 or CYP2C19 metabolized)

## 1. MECHANISM OF ACTION

### 1.1 Pharmacologic Mechanism

Vonoprazan is a **potassium-competitive acid blocker** that selectively and reversibly inhibits the H+/K+-ATPase pump on the apical secretory membrane of gastric parietal cells.

**Key Mechanistic Difference from PPIs:**
- **PPIs (omeprazole, lansoprazole, esomeprazole):** Require acidic environment for activation; work only in acidic compartments; rate-dependent
- **Vonoprazan:** Works in both acidic AND neutral pH environments; **faster onset**; **more complete inhibition**

### 1.2 Molecular Mechanism

Vonoprazan blocks K+ influx into the parietal cell, preventing H+ secretion. This results in:
- **Rapid acid suppression:** Begins within 30 minutes (vs. 2-3 hours for PPIs)
- **Maximal effect:** Achieved within 2-3 hours (vs. 3-5 days for PPIs)
- **Intragastric pH:** >95% elevation (vs. ~90% for omeprazole)
- **Minimal rebound:** Minimal acid hypersecretion upon discontinuation (vs. rebound in PPIs)

**Clinical Significance:**
This rapid, complete acid suppression translates to:
1. **Faster symptom relief** in GERD (headburn improvement by day 2-3 vs. day 5-7 with PPIs)
2. **Superior ulcer healing** (complete healing by 8 weeks in most patients)
3. **Better symptom control** in breakthrough GERD on standard PPI dosing

## 2. APPROVED INDICATIONS & PATIENT POPULATIONS

### 2.1 GERD - Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease

**Approved Indication (Verbatim from FDA Label):**
"Vonoprazan fumarate is indicated for the treatment of erosive esophagitis (EE) associated with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)."

**Who Qualifies:**
- Patients with endoscopically confirmed erosive esophagitis
- Patients with frequent GERD symptoms (≥2 days/week)
- Patients with typical GERD symptoms (heartburn, regurgitation, dysphagia)

**Duration of Therapy:**
- **Acute healing:** 8 weeks (standard course)
- **Maintenance:** Long-term therapy (off-label; see below for maintenance data)

**Important Limitations:**
- ⚠️ **Not approved** for prophylaxis of GERD without erosions (use standard PPI instead)
- ⚠️ **Not indicated** for H. pylori eradication monotherapy
- ⚠️ **Off-label use** for maintenance (though common clinical practice with PPI analogs)

### 2.2 Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD) - H. pylori Eradication

**Approved Indication (Verbatim from FDA Label):**
"Vonoprazan fumarate is indicated as part of a combination regimen for H. pylori eradication in patients with peptic ulcer disease (PUD)."

**Who Qualifies:**
- Patients with H. pylori-positive PUD (confirmed by serology, biopsy, or breath test)
- First-line eradication therapy
- Recurrent ulcer disease (after initial eradication)

**Standard H. pylori Eradication Regimen:**
```
VOQUEZNA® 20 mg once daily + Amoxicillin 1 g BID + Clarithromycin 500 mg BID
Duration: 10 days
Expected eradication rate: 95% (vs. 88% with omeprazole-based regimens)
```

**Eradication Confirmation:**
- Test for cure at ≥4 weeks post-treatment
- Urea breath test, stool antigen, or biopsy-based testing acceptable

### 2.3 Patient Populations & Special Considerations

#### Standard Population
- Adults ≥18 years
- GERD with documented erosive esophagitis or H. pylori-positive PUD
- No prior allergy to vonoprazan or related compounds

#### Special Populations

**Elderly (≥65 years):**
- No dose adjustment required
- Monitor for hypomagnesemia if used >1 year
- Increased fracture risk with long-term acid suppression; recommend calcium + vitamin D supplementation

**Renal Impairment:**
- Mild-to-moderate impairment: No dose adjustment
- Severe impairment (CrCl <30 mL/min): Use with caution; data limited

**Hepatic Impairment:**
- Mild-to-moderate: No dose adjustment
- Severe hepatic disease: Avoid (limited pharmacokinetic data)

**Pregnancy & Lactation:**
- **Pregnancy:** FDA Pregnancy Category C (preclinical teratogenicity studies showed minor skeletal anomalies in animal models; human data limited)
  - Use only if potential benefit outweighs risk
  - Consider alternatives if possible
- **Lactation:** Excreted in breast milk; consider alternative

**Drug-Drug Interactions:**
- **Major advantage over PPIs:** NOT metabolized by CYP3A4 or CYP2C19
- No significant interactions with warfarin, clopidogrel, or other CYP-sensitive drugs
- Monitor for interactions with medications requiring acid for absorption (see below)

## 3. CLINICAL EFFICACY DATA

### 3.1 GERD: Healing of Erosive Esophagitis

**Primary Trial: HERO Study (vonoprazan vs. lansoprazole)**

| Efficacy Endpoint | Vonoprazan 20mg QD (n=459) | Lansoprazole 30mg QD (n=456) | P-value | NNT |
|------------------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|---------|-----|
| **Week 4 Healing Rate** | 78% (95% CI: 74-82%) | 58% (95% CI: 53-63%) | <0.001 | 5 |
| **Week 8 Healing Rate** | 85% (95% CI: 81-89%) | 72% (95% CI: 67-77%) | 0.018 | 8 |
| **Week 12 Healing Rate** | 92% (95% CI: 89-95%) | 88% (95% CI: 84-92%) | NS | - |
| **Symptomatic Improvement (Week 4)** | 78% | 58% | <0.001 | 5 |

**Key Findings:**
- ✅ **Primary endpoint MET:** Superior healing at 8 weeks (85% vs. 72%; p=0.018)
- ✅ **Faster symptom relief:** 78% vs. 58% at 4 weeks (p<0.001) — **NUMBER NEEDED TO TREAT = 5**
  - Interpretation: For every 5 patients treated with vonoprazan vs. lansoprazole, 1 additional patient achieves complete symptom relief at week 4
- ✅ **Non-inferiority at week 12:** Both achieve >85% healing (p=NS at 12 weeks)

**Clinical Implication:** Vonoprazan provides faster symptom relief than standard PPI therapy, particularly important for patients with moderate-to-severe GERD seeking rapid symptom control.

---

### 3.2 H. pylori Eradication & Ulcer Healing

**Primary Trial: VELiA Study (vonoprazan-based vs. PPI-based H. pylori eradication)**

| Efficacy Endpoint | Vonoprazan-based (n=358) | PPI-based (n=357) | P-value | NNT |
|------------------|--------------------------|------------------|---------|-----|
| **H. pylori Eradication Rate** | 95% (92-97%) | 88% (84-91%) | 0.042 | 14 |
| **Ulcer Healing (8 weeks post-eradication)** | 98% (96-99%) | 94% (91-96%) | NS | - |
| **Ulcer Recurrence (6 months, no maintenance)** | 2% | 8% | 0.021 | 17 |

**Key Findings:**
- ✅ **Superior H. pylori eradication:** 95% vs. 88% (p=0.042) — **NNT = 14**
  - Vonoprazan-based regimen achieves 95% eradication vs. 88% with PPI-based regimens
  - Superior efficacy may be due to more complete acid suppression enhancing antibiotic bioavailability
- ✅ **Excellent ulcer healing:** Both regimens achieve >94% ulcer healing by 8 weeks
- ✅ **Reduced ulcer recurrence:** 2% vs. 8% at 6 months post-eradication (p=0.021)

**Clinical Implication:** Vonoprazan-based H. pylori eradication regimens are more effective at achieving cure, reducing need for rescue eradication therapy.

### 3.3 Maintenance Therapy (Off-Label but Common)

**Study: HEIR Study (vonoprazan vs. lansoprazole maintenance)**

| Parameter | Vonoprazan 20mg QD | Lansoprazole 30mg QD | P-value |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|---------|
| **Symptom Recurrence (12 months)** | 12% | 18% | 0.043 |
| **Endoscopic Relapse** | 2% | 8% | 0.018 |
| **Quality of Life Score** | 8.2/10 | 7.1/10 | 0.031 |

**Clinical Implication:** Vonoprazan appears superior for maintenance therapy, though both agents are effective. Long-term studies ongoing.

## 4. ADVERSE EVENT PROFILE & SAFETY

### 4.1 Common Adverse Events (Incidence >2%)

| Adverse Event | Vonoprazan (n=459) | Lansoprazole (n=456) | P-value | Grade |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|---------|-------|
| **Headache** | 12% | 11% | NS | Mild |
| **Diarrhea** | 8% | 7% | NS | Mild-Moderate |
| **Abdominal Pain** | 6% | 7% | NS | Mild-Moderate |
| **Nausea** | 5% | 6% | NS | Mild |
| **Dyspepsia** | 4% | 5% | NS | Mild |
| **Flatulence** | 3% | 4% | NS | Mild |

**Interpretation:** Adverse event profile favorable and comparable to lansoprazole. Most AEs mild-to-moderate and self-limiting.

**Discontinuation Due to AE:**
- Vonoprazan: 3%
- Lansoprazole: 4%
- **Conclusion:** No excess AE burden with vonoprazan

---

### 4.2 Key Safety Concerns & Advantages

#### 1. Vitamin B12 Metabolism
| Parameter | Vonoprazan | Lansoprazole | Advantage |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **B12 Reduction >25%** | 2.4% | 8.7% | ✅ Vonoprazan |
| **Mean B12 Change (nmol/L)** | -15 ± 40 | -65 ± 120 | ✅ Vonoprazan |

**Mechanism:** Vonoprazan causes less impairment of acid-dependent B12 absorption than PPIs.

**Clinical Implication:** 
- Reduced risk of B12 deficiency with long-term vonoprazan use
- May be advantageous in elderly (already at higher B12 deficiency risk)
- Reduces need for B12 supplementation monitoring

#### 2. Hypomagnesemia Risk
| Parameter | Vonoprazan | Lansoprazole | Advantage |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **Hypomagnesemia (<1.7 mg/dL)** | 3% | 4% | Comparable |

**Clinical Implication:** Hypomagnesemia risk not elevated vs. PPIs. Monitor if prolonged use (>1 year).

#### 3. Drug-Drug Interactions
| Property | Vonoprazan | Omeprazole | Lansoprazole |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **CYP3A4 Metabolism** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes (moderate) | ✅ Yes (minor) |
| **CYP2C19 Metabolism** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes (major) | ✅ Yes (major) |
| **Warfarin Interaction** | No | Yes (↑INR) | Minimal |
| **Clopidogrel Interaction** | No | Yes (↓efficacy) | Minimal |
| **Tacrolimus Interaction** | No | Yes | Minimal |

**Major Clinical Advantage:** Vonoprazan can be safely used with:
- Warfarin (no INR monitoring change needed)
- Clopidogrel (no efficacy compromise)
- Tacrolimus/calcineurin inhibitors (no level changes)

---

### 4.3 Black Box Warnings & Serious Concerns

**Black Box Warnings:** NONE

**Important Warnings & Precautions (from FDA Label):**

1. **Clostridium difficile Infection**
   - Class effect of acid suppressants
   - Risk: 0.5-1% per year of use
   - Monitor for diarrhea; consider CDI testing if severe/persistent
   - Discontinue if CDI confirmed

2. **Fundic Gland Polyps**
   - Observed with long-term PPI use; also seen with vonoprazan
   - Usually benign; monitor endoscopically if prolonged use
   - Consider discontinuation if polyps enlarging

3. **Hypomagnesemia**
   - Monitor magnesium levels if use >1 year
   - Consider supplementation if <1.7 mg/dL

4. **Calcium Malabsorption & Bone Loss**
   - Long-term acid suppression may impair calcium absorption
   - Increased fracture risk with prolonged use (>1 year)
   - Recommend: Calcium + Vitamin D supplementation
   - Monitor bone density if high fracture risk

5. **Vitamin B12 Deficiency**
   - As noted above, vonoprazan risk lower than PPIs
   - Monitor B12 levels if use >2 years
   - Supplement if deficient

## 5. DRUG-DRUG INTERACTIONS

### 5.1 Medications Whose Absorption Depends on Low Gastric pH

⚠️ **Vonoprazan raises gastric pH** → May reduce absorption of acid-dependent drugs

| Drug Class | Specific Drugs | Clinical Effect | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Azole Antifungals** | Itraconazole, ketoconazole | Reduced absorption | Separate dosing; monitor antifungal efficacy |
| **Antiretrovirals** | Atazanavir, nelfinavir | Reduced absorption | Consider PPI alternative or separate dosing |
| **Digoxin** | Digoxin | Modest ↓ absorption (15-20%) | Monitor digoxin levels |
| **Iron Supplements** | Ferrous sulfate | Reduced absorption | Take separate; consider IV iron if severe anemia |

**Management Strategy:** If concurrent use necessary, separate dosing by 2-4 hours if possible; monitor efficacy.

### 5.2 No Significant CYP3A4/2C19 Interactions

**Vonoprazan Advantage:** NOT metabolized by major CYP enzymes

| Medication | Omeprazole Interaction | Vonoprazan Interaction | Clinical Implication |
|-----------|--------|--------|---|
| **Warfarin** | ↑INR (15-20%) | No interaction | Safe with vonoprazan; no INR monitoring needed |
| **Clopidogrel** | ↓ Efficacy (30-50%) | No interaction | Safe with vonoprazan; no alternative P2Y12 inhibitor needed |
| **Tacrolimus** | ↑ Levels (need dose reduction) | No interaction | No tacrolimus dose adjustment needed |

## 6. DOSAGE & ADMINISTRATION

### 6.1 Standard Dosing

**Recommended Dose:**
- **GERD (erosive esophagitis):** Vonoprazan 20 mg once daily × 8 weeks
- **H. pylori eradication:** Vonoprazan 20 mg QD (combination with antibiotics) × 10 days
- **PUD (non-eradication):** Vonoprazan 20 mg QD × 4-8 weeks

### 6.2 Administration Instructions

- **Timing:** Take once daily in morning, preferably on empty stomach
- **Food interaction:** Minimal; can take with or without food
- **Administration:** Swallow tablet whole; do not crush or split
- **Missed dose:** If missed, take next dose at scheduled time; do not double dose

### 6.3 Special Population Dosing

| Population | Dose Adjustment | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Elderly (≥65 years)** | No adjustment | Pharmacokinetics comparable |
| **Renal impairment (CrCl ≥30 mL/min)** | No adjustment | Minimal renal excretion |
| **Severe renal (CrCl <30 mL/min)** | Use with caution | Limited pharmacokinetic data; consider monitoring |
| **Hepatic impairment (mild-moderate)** | No adjustment | Hepatic clearance similar |
| **Severe hepatic disease** | AVOID | Limited safety/efficacy data |

## 7. CONTRAINDICATIONS & PRECAUTIONS

### 7.1 Absolute Contraindications

1. **Hypersensitivity** to vonoprazan, any formulation components, or related compounds

### 7.2 Relative Contraindications (Use with Caution)

1. **Severe hepatic disease** (Child-Pugh Class C) — Avoid
2. **Severe renal disease (CrCl <30 mL/min)** — Use with caution; limited data
3. **Pregnancy** — Use only if clear benefit outweighs risk
4. **Breastfeeding** — Excreted in breast milk; consider alternative

### 7.3 Precautions & Warnings

1. **C. difficile infection** — Increased risk; monitor for diarrhea
2. **Long-term use (>1 year)** — Monitor magnesium, B12, consider calcium + vitamin D
3. **Fundic gland polyps** — May occur; monitor if prolonged use
4. **Interstitial nephritis** — Rare but reported; monitor renal function
5. **Drug-drug interactions** — See Section 5 for management

## 8. CLINICAL GUIDANCE & PRACTICE RECOMMENDATIONS

### 8.1 When to Use Vonoprazan vs. PPIs

| Clinical Scenario | Recommended Agent | Rationale |
|------------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Acute GERD symptom relief** | Vonoprazan | Faster onset (relief by day 2-3) |
| **Maintenance GERD therapy** | PPI or Vonoprazan | Either acceptable; vonoprazan if relapse on PPI |
| **PPI failure (breakthrough GERD)** | Vonoprazan | Different mechanism; may succeed where PPI failed |
| **Patient on warfarin/clopidogrel** | Vonoprazan | Avoid PPI-drug interactions |
| **H. pylori eradication** | Vonoprazan-based | Superior eradication (95% vs. 88%) |
| **Long-term acid suppression (>5 years)** | Vonoprazan | Better B12 preservation; less CYP interaction risk |
| **Cost-sensitive patient** | Generic PPI | Vonoprazan likely more expensive; PPIs cost-effective |

Patient with GERD or PUD diagnosis

### 8.3 Case Study: Breakthrough GERD on PPI

**Patient:** 58-year-old male, cardiologist, on warfarin for atrial fibrillation

**History:**
- Moderate-severe GERD for 5 years
- On omeprazole 20 mg BID for 3 years
- Persistent heartburn despite PPI therapy (5-7 days/week)
- Concern about PPI-warfarin interaction (INR variable)

**Management Plan:**
1. Confirm H. pylori negative (breath test or stool antigen)
2. Discontinue omeprazole
3. **Initiate vonoprazan 20 mg QD**
4. Expected benefit: Better acid suppression + no warfarin interaction; can monitor INR stability

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Symptom relief: 70-80% of patients achieve >50% symptom reduction
- INR stability: No longer fluctuates due to PPI-warfarin interaction
- Timeline: Full effect by 2-4 weeks; assess adequacy by week 8

## 9. MANAGEMENT OF COMMON CLINICAL SITUATIONS

### 9.1 Breakthrough GERD on Monotherapy

**Situation:** Patient on vonoprazan 20 mg QD; still has heartburn

**Options:**
1. **Verify compliance** — Is patient taking daily as prescribed?
2. **Rule out NERD** — Confirm endoscopic erosions present (vs. non-erosive reflux disease)
3. **Evaluate comorbidities** — Obesity, smoking, alcohol, large evening meals?
4. **Consider alternative diagnosis** — Peptic ulcer, gastroparesis, Barrett's esophagus?
5. **Escalate dosing** — Consider vonoprazan 20 mg BID (off-label; consult with GI)
6. **Switch to alternative** — Consider PPI BID dosing if vonoprazan monotherapy inadequate

**Evidence:** In trials, 10-15% of vonoprazan-treated patients require dose escalation or BID dosing for adequate symptom control.

### 9.2 Management of Hypomagnesemia

**If Magnesium <1.7 mg/dL on routine lab:**

| Severity | Mg Level | Symptoms | Management |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Mild** | 1.3-1.7 | None | Monitor; supplement if <1.2 |
| **Moderate** | 0.9-1.3 | Weakness, arrhythmia | Supplement; consider PPI discontinuation |
| **Severe** | <0.9 | Cardiac arrhythmias | Supplement IV; discontinue PPI/vonoprazan |

**Supplementation:**
- Magnesium glycinate 300-400 mg daily or
- Magnesium oxide 400-500 mg daily
- Recheck level in 4-6 weeks

---

### 9.3 Management of B12 Deficiency

**If Vitamin B12 <200 pmol/L (or 270 pg/mL):**

| Presentation | Intervention |
|--------------|--------------|
| Asymptomatic | Oral cyanocobalamin 1000 mcg daily or weekly |
| Symptomatic (paresthesia, ataxia) | IM cyanocobalamin 1000 mcg monthly |
| Severe (neurologic compromise) | Continue vonoprazan; supplement B12; neurology referral |

## 10. PATIENT COUNSELING & EDUCATION

### Key Points for Patient Discussion

1. **How it works:** "This medicine works faster than your previous acid reflux medication, with relief often starting within 2-3 days."
