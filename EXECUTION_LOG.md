SECTION 3: MECHANISM OF ACTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Molecular Target: H+/K+-ATPase Pump

HOW IT WORKS:

Step 1: Binding
Vonoprazan binds directly to the H+/K+-ATPase pump on gastric parietal cells 
in a potassium-competitive manner (hence the name).

Step 2: Inhibition
The drug competitively displaces potassium ions from the pump's binding site, 
preventing acid secretion.

Step 3: Effect
Acid secretion is blocked immediately, unlike PPIs which require:
  1. Activation in acidic environment (2-4 hours)
  2. Covalent binding to cysteine residues
  3. 5-7 days for full effect

Vonoprazan Timeline:
  • Effect onset: 30 minutes
  • Peak effect: 2-3 hours
  • Full acid suppression: 4-6 hours
  • Steady state: 1-2 days

PPI Timeline (comparator):
  • Effect onset: 2-4 hours (requires activation)
  • Peak effect: 3-5 days (after multiple doses)
  • Full acid suppression: 5-7 days
  • Steady state: 5-7 days

CLINICAL ADVANTAGE:
Vonoprazan achieves therapeutic acid suppression 30-40x faster than PPIs, 
meaning faster symptom relief and faster healing (2-4 weeks vs 4-8 weeks).
