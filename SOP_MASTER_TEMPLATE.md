## SECTION 4: QUALITY GATES (10 Mandatory Gates)

**Every SOP must include 10 quality gates. Here's the standard framework:**

### GATE 1: Completeness ✅
- **Objective:** Verify all required sections are present
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - All 12 sections drafted (see structure above)
  - No placeholder text ("TBD", "TODO")
  - Word count within target range (±10%)
- **Approval Authority:** QA Reviewer
- **Timeline:** Should pass by Day 1-2
- **Risk if Failed:** Task cannot proceed; restart drafting

### GATE 2: Source Traceability ✅
- **Objective:** Every claim must be traceable to a credible source
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Every factual claim has a citation
  - Sources are ranked (FDA > EMA > published RCT > observational)
  - Citation format consistent (author, year, journal)
  - 100% substantiation rate (zero uncited claims)
- **Approval Authority:** Compliance Officer + Medical Specialist
- **Timeline:** Days 2-3
- **Risk if Failed:** Gate 3 cannot pass; compliance violation

### GATE 3: On-Label Verification ✅
- **Objective:** Confirm every claim stays within FDA label boundaries
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Claims reviewed against FDA-approved label (verbatim)
  - No off-label indications mentioned
  - No off-label populations (unless supported by published data)
  - Comparative claims supported by head-to-head trials
- **Approval Authority:** Regulatory Affairs + Compliance Officer
- **Timeline:** Days 2-3
- **Risk if Failed:** Regulatory violation; cannot release

### GATE 4: Fair-Balance Assessment ✅
- **Objective:** Ensure benefits and risks are presented proportionally
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Fair-balance score ≥80 (quantified)
  - Benefit language matches safety language (e.g., "benefits include X" → "risks include Y")
  - Absolute benefit thresholds presented (e.g., "50% vs. 30% = 20% absolute reduction")
  - Risk minimization strategies documented
- **Approval Authority:** Compliance Officer
- **Timeline:** Days 3-4
- **Risk if Failed:** ABPI code violation; FDA warning letter risk

### GATE 5: Medical Accuracy ✅
- **Objective:** Independent clinical expert validates scientific content
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - No scientific errors detected
  - Statistical interpretation correct (p-values, CI, NNT)
  - Clinical significance assessment accurate
  - Real-world applicability confirmed
- **Approval Authority:** External Clinical Expert (not task team)
- **Timeline:** Days 3-4
- **Risk if Failed:** Credibility loss; physician distrust

### GATE 6: Regulatory Compliance ✅
- **Objective:** Confirm alignment with FDA, EMA, ABPI, UCPMP standards
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - No FDA warning letter precedents violated
  - ABPI Code Section 1-3 compliance verified
  - EMA requirements met (if applicable)
  - UCPMP quality standards exceeded
- **Approval Authority:** Regulatory Affairs + Legal
- **Timeline:** Days 4-5
- **Risk if Failed:** Regulatory action risk

### GATE 7: Legal Review ✅
- **Objective:** Liability & legal exposure assessment
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - No defamatory comparative claims
  - No contractual obligation violations
  - Liability exposure: LOW
  - Insurance coverage confirmed for any remaining risk
- **Approval Authority:** Legal Counsel
- **Timeline:** Days 4-5
- **Risk if Failed:** Litigation risk; insurance denial

### GATE 8: Formatting & Structure ✅
- **Objective:** Professional presentation standards
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Consistent formatting (fonts, spacing, numbering)
  - Professional layout (headers, footers, page numbers)
  - Table/figure quality high
  - Bibliography complete & correctly formatted (AMA or Vancouver)
  - No typos or grammatical errors
- **Approval Authority:** QA Reviewer
- **Timeline:** Days 5-6
- **Risk if Failed:** Professional reputation impact

### GATE 9: Audit Trail ✅
- **Objective:** Complete documentation for compliance & audit
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Decision log complete (all major decisions documented)
  - Approval signatures collected (8 minimum)
  - Change log filled (who changed what, when, why)
  - Evidence library archived (all sources stored)
  - Audit metadata complete (creator, date, version)
- **Approval Authority:** Compliance Officer
- **Timeline:** Days 5-6
- **Risk if Failed:** Audit failure; cannot defend compliance

### GATE 10: Final Release ✅
- **Objective:** Executive sign-off & release authorization
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - All 9 prior gates passed (100% non-negotiable)
  - CMO review completed (final read-through)
  - Executive decision: APPROVED vs. REWORK vs. REJECT
  - Release authorization signed
  - Stakeholder notification sent
- **Approval Authority:** Chief Medical Officer (ULTIMATE)
- **Timeline:** 1 day
- **Risk if Failed:** Cannot release; must address prior gate failures

## SECTION 5: QUALITY GATE FRAMEWORK (Detailed)

### Gate Ownership & Escalation

```
Gate 1 (Completeness)
├─ Owner: QA Reviewer
├─ Escalation: Project Manager (if failed)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Restart drafting (1 day typically)

Gate 2 (Source Traceability)
├─ Owner: Compliance Officer + Medical Specialist
├─ Escalation: VP Regulatory (if multiple sources fail)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Add missing citations (2-6 hours)

Gate 3 (On-Label Verification)
├─ Owner: Regulatory Affairs
├─ Escalation: VP Regulatory → CMO (if off-label drift)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Remove off-label claims (4-8 hours)

Gate 4 (Fair-Balance Assessment)
├─ Owner: Compliance Officer
├─ Escalation: CMO (if score <70)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Revise language for balance (4-6 hours)

Gate 5 (Medical Accuracy)
├─ Owner: External Clinical Expert
├─ Escalation: CMO (if significant errors found)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Address expert feedback (4-8 hours)

Gate 6 (Regulatory Compliance)
├─ Owner: Regulatory Affairs + Legal
├─ Escalation: VP Regulatory + CMO (if warning letter risk)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Align with regulatory guidance (6-12 hours)

Gate 7 (Legal Review)
├─ Owner: Legal Counsel
├─ Escalation: General Counsel (if litigation risk)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Legal revisions (6-12 hours)

Gate 8 (Formatting & Structure)
├─ Owner: QA Reviewer
├─ Escalation: Project Manager (if major reformat needed)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Format corrections (2-4 hours)

Gate 9 (Audit Trail)
├─ Owner: Compliance Officer
├─ Escalation: Compliance Director (if documentation incomplete)
└─ REWORK POLICY: Complete documentation (2-4 hours)

Gate 10 (Final Release)
├─ Owner: Chief Medical Officer (ULTIMATE AUTHORITY)
├─ Escalation: CFO/CEO (if strategic concern)
└─ APPROVAL POLICY: All prior gates must pass (non-negotiable)
```

### Gate Passage Criteria & Metrics

**PASS Definition:** Gate passes when acceptance criteria are 100% met  
**CONDITIONAL PASS:** Not allowed — gates are binary (PASS or REWORK)  
**REWORK:** Identified failures → specific remediation → re-evaluation  
**ESCALATION:** Blocker preventing rework → escalate per matrix above

## SECTION 7: RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

**Every SOP must identify 5+ risks and mitigation approaches:**

```
Risk 1: Clinical Data Completeness Gaps
├─ Severity: MEDIUM
├─ Probability: 30% (based on Phase 1 experience)
├─ Impact: Task delay (2-3 days) if data unavailable
├─ Root Cause: Incomplete FDA submissions or off-label studies
├─ Mitigation:
│  ├─ Primary: Use FDA CDER submission package (most complete)
│  ├─ Backup: Published peer-reviewed trials
│  ├─ Fallback: EMA Assessment Report (alternative source)
│  └─ Pre-gate: Data completeness check before Gate 2
└─ Owner: Medical Specialist

Risk 2: Regulatory Compliance Violation
├─ Severity: HIGH
├─ Probability: 5% (gates designed to prevent)
├─ Impact: FDA warning letter, product recall, legal action
├─ Root Cause: Off-label claims, unfair balance, false comparatives
├─ Mitigation:
│  ├─ Primary: 3-person compliance review (Gates 3, 4, 6)
│  ├─ Backup: External regulatory consultant if any doubt
│  ├─ Escalation: CMO sign-off before release
│  └─ Precedent: Reference prior warning letters
└─ Owner: VP Regulatory

Risk 3: Source Bias (Manufacturer-Sponsored Studies)
├─ Severity: MEDIUM
├─ Probability: 20%
├─ Impact: Medical accuracy concerns, physician skepticism
├─ Root Cause: Over-reliance on company-sponsored trials
├─ Mitigation:
│  ├─ Primary: Prioritize independent RCTs (published)
│  ├─ Backup: Require third-party meta-analyses
│  ├─ Transparency: Clearly disclose study sponsorship
│  └─ Balancing: Include safety data from all sources
└─ Owner: CMO

Risk 4: Fair-Balance Assessment Subjectivity
├─ Severity: MEDIUM
├─ Probability: 35%
├─ Impact: Different assessors might rate "fair-balance" differently
├─ Root Cause: Subjective interpretation of "balance"
├─ Mitigation:
│  ├─ Primary: Fair-Balance Calculator (quantified scale 0-100)
│  ├─ Backup: GSK Fair-Balance Method (3-point checklist)
│  ├─ Escalation: If score <70, escalate to CMO
│  └─ Documentation: Show exact benefit/risk ratio numbers
└─ Owner: Compliance Officer

Risk 5: Medical Accuracy Expert Unavailability
├─ Severity: LOW
├─ Probability: 10%
├─ Impact: Gate 5 delay (2-3 days)
├─ Root Cause: Expert busy with other projects
├─ Mitigation:
│  ├─ Primary: Designate 2 expert reviewers (redundancy)
│  ├─ Backup: Contract external clinical reviewer ($5K per review)
│  ├─ Timeline: Submit for review by Day 3 (early, provides buffer)
│  └─ SLA: Expert reviews within 48 hours (contractual)
```

## SECTION 8: DECISION FRAMEWORK & AUTO-LEARNING

**How decisions are documented for continuous improvement:**

### Decision Log Template

```json
{
  "taskId": "task-6-product-profile",
  "date": "2026-06-14",
  "decision": "Clinical evidence selection",
  "decisionPoint": "Step 1 — Which trials to include?",
  "options": [
    "Include only Phase 3 RCTs (n>100)",
    "Include Phase 2 + Phase 3 + observational",
    "Include manufacturer + independent studies"
  ],
  "chosenOption": "Include Phase 3 RCTs only",
  "reasoning": "Phase 3 RCTs are gold standard; Phase 2 data less robust",
  "evidence": "FDA guidance on efficacy claims requires Phase 3 support",
  "outcome": "ACCEPTED — Decision approved by Gate 2 & 5",
  "qualityScore": "A+ (99% confidence)",
  "learningPoint": "Phase 3 priority rule applies across all 45 tasks",
  "nextBatchApplication": "Task 7 (Competitive Analysis) — document trial hierarchy upfront",
  "automationOpportunity": "Create automated trial selection algorithm based on Phase 3 > Phase 2"
}
```

### Weekly Learning Loop (Friday 5 PM)

**Process:**
1. Aggregate all decision logs from the week
2. Identify recurring patterns (3+ occurrences = pattern)
3. Extract learning points (what worked well)
4. Identify improvement areas (what could be better)
5. Document recommendations for next batch

**Example Output:**

```
PATTERN: Trial selection takes longer than expected (avg 4 hours)
ROOT CAUSE: No pre-defined trial hierarchy; reviewers deciding ad-hoc
LEARNING: GSK method (Phase 3 > Phase 2 > observational) works well
IMPROVEMENT: Encode trial hierarchy into Pharma-Product-Profile-Synthesis-v1 skill
IMPACT: Estimated 2-hour time savings per task (=20 hours across Batch 2)
BATCH 2 ACTION: Apply pre-defined hierarchy to Task 7-10 drafting
```
