## Key Review Findings

### Product Appraisal

- It is one Medico-Marketing activity, not the prime or sole Medical Department activity.
- Its purpose includes comprehensive Go/No-Go support and can require clinical, preclinical, regulatory, patent, PV, HEOR, pricing, supply, operational, strategic-fit, and commercial inputs.
- The supplied skill includes a weighted 100-point appraisal, red-flag overrides, source triangulation, and detailed decision framework.
- The supplied SOP defines a broad 23-section product-appraisal procedure and cross-functional responsibilities.
- Alignment with the owner-supplied meta-prompt is **in progress**: the core appraisal controls are present, but the current implementation still needs explicit ROI framing, better learning loops, and tighter packaging of Go/No-Go outputs for newer candidates.

### Product Monograph

- The supplied SOP defines a detailed scientific monograph and prescribing-information structure, revision triggers, distribution, and controlled-copy expectations.
- The supplied skill adds substantial modern governance: label boundaries, balanced claims, compliance frameworks, claim substantiation, fair comparisons, design standards, derivative content, and approval checklist.
- The final activity package must explicitly distinguish scientific/non-promotional monographs from HCP promotional or mixed-use monographs.
- Alignment with the owner-supplied meta-prompt is **in progress**: the scientific structure is present, but prose style, bibliography format, and controlled learning loops still need refinement.

### CME Slides

- The supplied SOP defines scientific, non-commercially biased CME slide preparation, privacy protections, content structure, revision triggers, distribution, and slide-design standards.
- CME Slides must remain separate from Product Training Slides because their audience, purpose, bias controls, and distribution differ.
- A draft CME skill proposal now exists in the enterprise-architecture docs and should be reviewed before implementation.

### Product Training Slides

- The supplied skill defines a compliance-first field-force training program with approved-source dependencies, speaker notes, FAQs, escalation, role play, assessment, and certification.
- A dedicated Product Training Slides SOP is still required.

## Source Request Rule

For each new activity:

1. Check this register.
2. Request the owner's existing SOP, skill, templates, and checklists.
3. Perform a gap assessment.
4. Prepare proposed additions or a draft package.
5. Present it for owner review.
6. Implement only after approval.

## Cross-Activity Meta-Prompt Rule

The owner-supplied meta-prompt is treated as a live learning reference for both product monograph and product appraisal work. It does not replace the source SOP and skill package, but it should be consulted to refine style, quality, and validation habits whenever those packages are used.
