# SOP for Clinical Summary Preparation

**Document status:** Draft for owner review  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Activity:** Clinical Summary Preparation  
**Owning domain:** Medical Writing / Clinical Research Support  
**Primary citation style:** Vancouver style  

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines how ClinCommand OS prepares a clinical summary that condenses clinical evidence into a clear, accurate, and decision-ready document.

The purpose is to help medical, clinical, and governance teams understand a study, a body of studies, a molecule, or a clinical question without losing scientific fidelity.

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to summaries of:

- clinical studies;
- development programs;
- efficacy and safety evidence;
- comparative evidence;
- post-marketing evidence where relevant;
- therapy-area evidence summaries;
- executive clinical briefings;
- decision-support summaries for internal review.

This SOP does not replace a full CSR SOP, publication SOP, monograph SOP, or appraisal SOP.

## 3. Reference Style

All scientific statements in the clinical summary must be referenced in **Vancouver style**.

Rules:

1. Use numbered in-text citations in sequence.
2. Keep the main text free of raw URLs.
3. Use a numbered reference list at the end.
4. Prefer primary literature, official study reports, guidelines, registries, and approved labels.
5. If a claim is drawn from a secondary source, identify that source transparently and do not present it as primary evidence.

## 4. Responsibilities

### Clinical summary lead

- define the question the summary must answer;
- define the evidence boundary;
- ensure the summary reflects the correct development stage or clinical context;
- maintain citation discipline.

### Source subagent

- retrieve protocols, abstracts, full texts, registries, and relevant official documents;
- map each important conclusion to a source;
- identify missing data, inconsistent reporting, or source gaps.

### Summarization subagent

- convert the evidence into a concise, logical summary;
- preserve methods, results, limitations, and interpretation;
- avoid over-interpreting a single study;
- keep the tone scientific and neutral.

### QA subagent

- verify data points, denominators, effect directions, and terminology;
- check that the summary is not too long, too short, or overly promotional;
- confirm that citations support the exact claim being made.

### Compliance subagent

- check that the summary stays within the intended use;
- confirm privacy and confidentiality;
- confirm that no unsupported claims, prohibited promotional language, or misleading comparisons appear.

### Memory subagent

- store the evidence patterns, phrasing patterns, and recurring errors for future refinement.

## 5. Required Inputs

The request should include or allow discovery of:

- study name, program name, or clinical question;
- product or molecule;
- indication or disease area;
- target audience;
- geography;
- intended use;
- available source pack;
- study stage or evidence type;
- required length or format;
- whether tables or figures are needed.

## 6. Required Procedure

### Step 1: Intake and scope lock

- confirm what the summary must answer;
- define whether the summary is study-level, program-level, or therapeutic-area level;
- identify the output format and expected depth;
- list the key sources and the evidence boundary.

### Step 2: Source resolution

Resolve sources in this order:

1. owner-approved SOPs and skill files;
2. controlled repository materials;
3. approved project memory and architecture notes;
4. primary literature and official evidence sources;
5. external skill registries only for skill learning and pattern improvement.

### Step 3: Evidence extraction

Extract:

- objective;
- design;
- population;
- intervention;
- comparator;
- endpoints;
- statistical approach;
- key results;
- safety findings;
- limitations;
- clinical implication.

### Step 4: Summary architecture

The clinical summary should generally include:

- background and purpose;
- methods or evidence source description;
- key efficacy findings;
- key safety findings;
- limitations;
- interpretation;
- practical relevance;
- reference list.

### Step 5: Drafting

- keep language concise and exact;
- avoid narrative embellishment;
- use tables where they improve clarity;
- cite each numerical or clinical claim;
- preserve the original meaning of the evidence.

### Step 6: Scientific QA

Check:

- accuracy of numerical values;
- sequence and consistency of citations;
- correct interpretation of results;
- no invented endpoints, subgroup claims, or causal language;
- no omission of important limitations.

### Step 7: Compliance QA

Check:

- intended-use compliance;
- confidentiality and privacy;
- fair-balance requirements where relevant;
- jurisdictional restrictions;
- no disguised promotion.

### Step 8: Refinement pass

Perform one refinement pass after QA to:

- improve readability;
- tighten sequence;
- improve section headings;
- normalize Vancouver references;
- remove redundancy.

### Step 9: Memory update

Record:

- which study types were easiest to summarize;
- which evidence fields caused errors;
- which wording patterns produced the cleanest summary;
- which reference patterns were most reliable.

## 7. Required Output Structure

Each clinical summary should contain:

1. Title
2. Objective / question
3. Evidence scope
4. Study or evidence overview
5. Key findings
6. Safety findings
7. Limitations
8. Interpretation
9. Practical relevance
10. Vancouver references

## 8. Controls

- No unsupported inference.
- No unreferenced numerical claim.
- No mixing of raw notes with final text.
- No editorial language that sounds like promotion.
- No citation style drift away from Vancouver.
- No omission of safety or limitations when those materially affect interpretation.

## 9. Revision Triggers

This SOP must be reviewed when:

- new data materially changes the evidence;
- the source package changes;
- the intended audience changes;
- the jurisdiction changes;
- the clinical question changes;
- the summary repeatedly fails quality checks.

## 10. Training Requirement

Every AI subagent must complete supervised extraction, summarization, and QA at least once before it is considered reliable for this activity.

The learning loop must capture:

- source ranking;
- summary structure;
- citation accuracy;
- error patterns;
- improvements for the next draft.
