# One-Week Enterprise Architecture Delivery Plan

**Document status:** Accepted  
**Delivery window:** 2026-06-06 to 2026-06-12  
**Primary goal:** Produce an implementation-ready enterprise architecture baseline and prove it with one thin, end-to-end governed workflow.

## Week-One Outcome

By the end of the week, ClinCommand OS must have:

- an accepted product constitution and bounded enterprise scope;
- an agreed enterprise domain vocabulary;
- a Universal Object Registry specification;
- an initial relationship graph and event catalogue;
- a versioned SOP- and skill-gated workflow contract;
- an AI governance and multi-agent execution contract;
- a security, validation, and audit baseline;
- a documented assessment of existing assets;
- a working architecture skeleton or executable thin slice for Product Appraisal;
- an agreed backlog for the next delivery cycle.

## Scope Guardrail

The week is for architecture baseline and proof, not full enterprise completion. Work that does not reduce architectural uncertainty or prove the first governed workflow is deferred.

## Daily Plan

| Day | Focus | Required Deliverables | Exit Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Constitution and boundaries | Living-memory structure, constitution, decision log, delivery board, system context | Product purpose, boundaries, and principles accepted |
| Day 2 | Enterprise domain | Domain glossary, bounded contexts, core entities, relationship model | Major terms have one agreed meaning and owner |
| Day 3 | Enterprise kernel contracts | Universal object, event envelope, workflow execution, SOP and skill gates | Product Appraisal can be represented end to end on paper |
| Day 4 | AI, security, and validation | Agent contract, governance rules, memory policy, trust boundaries, validation strategy | AI actions and approvals are bounded and traceable |
| Day 5 | Existing-system assessment | Reuse/replace/retire matrix, gap assessment, migration principles | Candidate assets are classified without ambiguity |
| Day 6 | Thin-slice proof | Product Appraisal workflow skeleton with events, gates, and audit evidence | One governed workflow executes or is fully testable |
| Day 7 | Review and next cycle | Architecture review, risks, decisions, updated backlog, next sprint plan | Baseline accepted and implementation sequence approved |

## First Thin Slice: Product Appraisal

The proof must demonstrate:

1. A user requests a Product Appraisal.
2. The activity resolves its active workflow and SOP versions.
3. Required requester, contributor, and reviewer skills are checked.
4. Approved agents receive structured, bounded tasks.
5. Sources and generated artifacts retain provenance.
6. Validation findings are recorded.
7. A qualified human approves or rejects the output.
8. All material changes emit events and appear in the audit history.
9. The completed appraisal is linked to its product, indication, evidence, SOP, participants, and decisions.

## Delivery Gates

### Gate A: Coherence

- No critical concept has conflicting definitions.
- Module boundaries and ownership are explicit.
- Product Appraisal maps cleanly through all core contracts.

### Gate B: Governance

- SOP and skill gates cannot be bypassed through normal execution.
- AI cannot self-approve.
- Every material execution step is attributable and traceable.

### Gate C: Feasibility

- Existing assets are classified as reuse, adapt, replace, or retire.
- Major technical risks have owners and mitigations.
- The next implementation cycle is sized and ordered.

## Daily Working Rhythm

- Start of day: choose the day's three highest-value outcomes.
- Midday: record new decisions, risks, and assumption changes.
- End of day: update the Delivery Board, confirm exit gate, and set the next day's first task.
- No architectural decision remains only in chat or meeting notes.

## Success Measures

- 100% of critical decisions recorded.
- 100% of thin-slice steps linked to SOP, skills, evidence, events, and human accountability.
- Zero unresolved critical domain-definition conflicts.
- Existing-system assessment completed for all kernel-relevant modules.
- Next-cycle backlog accepted with dependencies and delivery gates.
