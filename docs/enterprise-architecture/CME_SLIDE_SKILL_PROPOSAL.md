# CME Slide Preparation Skill Proposal

**Document status:** Draft for owner review  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Purpose:** Define a CME-specific skill direction that aligns to the supplied SOP for Preparation of CME Slides and stays clearly separate from Product Training Slides.

## 1. Working Title

Scientific CME Slide Development and Validation

## 2. Activity Boundary

This skill applies to scientific CME slide decks intended for professional education. It is not a field-force training skill, not a promotional detailing skill, and not a product monograph.

The activity should remain:

- scientifically balanced;
- non-commercially biased;
- privacy-safe;
- reference-led;
- professionally presented;
- update-aware when scientific or safety information changes.

## 3. Source Basis

This proposal is aligned to:

- `SOP of Preparation of CME Slides.docx`
- the existing Product Training Slides skill only as a neighbouring control reference
- the owner-supplied meta-prompt for product monograph and appraisal quality discipline

## 4. Intended Use

The skill should support preparation of CME slide presentations for:

1. disease-state education
2. therapeutic-area education
3. product-focused scientific education when explicitly authorised
4. guideline and treatment-landscape discussion
5. medical director review and approval workflows

## 5. Required Capabilities

1. Convert a clinical or disease-state topic into a clean slide architecture.
2. Identify the core scientific message for each slide.
3. Separate background science from product-specific material.
4. Present benefit, risk, and limitation in balance.
5. Support every key statement with a source reference.
6. Avoid duplication across slides unless repetition improves learning.
7. Maintain privacy controls for patient, laboratory, imaging, and case-based material.
8. Flag revision triggers when safety, indications, or usage conditions change.
9. Preserve a simple, readable, non-crowded slide design.
10. Prepare speaker notes where required.

## 6. Mandatory Inputs

The skill should require:

- CME objective
- topic
- target audience
- geography
- duration
- approved source pack
- literature / guidelines list
- product information if product discussion is in scope
- privacy and consent status for case-based material
- template and branding requirements

## 7. Expected Outputs

1. slide blueprint
2. slide-by-slide content plan
3. source map
4. speaker notes
5. reference list
6. bias and fair-balance checklist
7. privacy review checklist
8. revision history recommendation

## 8. Critical Controls

1. No commercial bias for or against any product.
2. No unsupported superiority claim.
3. No absolute safety claim.
4. No identifiable patient material without permission.
5. References on applicable slides.
6. Summary and key points included.
7. Medical Director review completed before release.
8. Clear separation between scientific education and promotional content.

## 9. Draft Review Questions

1. Should the skill support disease-only CME decks, product-linked CME decks, or both?
2. Should the output be a slide blueprint only, or a full content plan with speaker notes?
3. Should the skill enforce a fixed slide order or allow topic-dependent ordering?
4. Should the skill include a standard privacy and consent checklist as a required output?
5. Should the skill be paired with a separate CME slide template standard?

## 10. Proposed Next Step

If approved, this proposal should be converted into a controlled CME skill package and linked to a validation workflow that tests:

1. scientific balance;
2. reference quality;
3. privacy protection;
4. readability;
5. revision-trigger handling;
6. separation from Product Training Slides.
