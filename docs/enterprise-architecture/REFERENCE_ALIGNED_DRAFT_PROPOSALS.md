## Proposal 1: CME Slide Preparation Skill

### Proposed Skill Name

Scientific CME Slide Development and Validation

### Positioning

This skill supports preparation of scientifically balanced, non-commercially biased CME slide presentations for defined professional audiences. It is separate from Product Training Slides and promotional HCP decks.

### Required Capabilities

- disease-background and pathophysiology synthesis;
- guideline and treatment-landscape interpretation;
- product scientific-information synthesis where relevant;
- evidence selection and slide-level citation;
- privacy-safe use of patient, laboratory, imaging, and case materials;
- balanced efficacy and safety presentation;
- non-commercial-bias assessment;
- scientific slide architecture and visual communication;
- revision impact assessment when safety, indication, or use conditions change.

### Mandatory Inputs

- CME objective, topic, audience, duration, and geography;
- current approved scientific source pack;
- applicable guidelines and literature;
- product information where product discussion is in scope;
- privacy and consent status for any patient-derived material;
- supplied CME Slide SOP and controlled template.

### Proposed Outputs

- slide blueprint;
- slide-by-slide scientific content;
- speaker notes;
- slide-level reference map;
- bias and fair-balance checklist;
- privacy review checklist;
- revision and distribution record.

### Critical Controls

- no commercial bias for or against a product;
- no unsupported or misleading scientific statement;
- identifiable patient data prohibited without documented permission;
- references included on applicable slides;
- content density and readability standards met;
- summary and key points included;
- Medical Director review and approval completed.

### Proposed Assessment

The skill holder should demonstrate the ability to convert a scientific topic into a balanced slide architecture, support every material statement, identify bias and privacy risks, and explain revision triggers.

## Proposal 2: Product Training Slides SOP

### Proposed SOP Title

Preparation, Review, Approval, Revision, and Distribution of Product Training Slides

### Proposed Purpose

Define the controlled process for developing field-force Product Training Slides that are scientifically accurate, aligned with the approved training manual and product monograph, compliant with local requirements, and effective for training, query handling, escalation, and certification.

### Proposed Scope

Applies to internal Product Training Slides for induction, launch readiness, refresher training, objection handling, FAQ handling, compliance coaching, role play, and assessment.

### Proposed Responsibilities

- Medical author prepares scientific and training content.
- Training owner defines learning objectives and assessment requirements.
- Medical Director reviews scientific accuracy and balance.
- Regulatory/legal/compliance reviewers assess claims and permitted use where required.
- Pharmacovigilance and quality owners review escalation content.
- QA maintains controlled copies and revision history.
- Trainers use only the effective approved version.

### Proposed Procedure

1. Confirm training purpose, audience, market, duration, and deployment method.
2. Resolve current approved source pack, including training manual, monograph, label, claims library, FAQ, safety, and escalation procedures.
3. Define learning objectives and assessment domains.
4. Build the slide blueprint and source map.
5. Draft disease, product, efficacy, safety, dosing, positioning, query-handling, escalation, and assessment modules.
6. Add speaker notes with what to say, what not to say, challenge handling, and escalation.
7. Validate on-label boundaries, fair balance, source support, AE/product-complaint escalation, and internal-use markings.
8. Complete medical and required cross-functional review.
9. Approve, version, distribute, train, assess, and certify.
10. Revise when source documents, claims, safety, indications, regulations, or training needs change.

### Proposed Critical Controls

- only approved source pack used;
- no proactive off-label messaging;
- no unsupported superiority or absolute safety claims;
- query and AE/product-complaint escalation explicit;
- assessment and certification included;
- current approved deck only available to trainers and field force.

## Proposal 3: Product Monograph Package Reconciliation

The supplied Product Monograph SOP and skill appear to address overlapping but not identical intended uses.

Before implementation, the owner should choose whether ClinCommand supports:

1. a scientific, non-promotional Product Monograph;
2. an HCP promotional Product Monograph;
3. separate activity variants for both.

The recommended architecture is separate controlled variants because label boundaries, compliance review, prescribing-information requirements, distribution, claims controls, and derivative-content use differ materially.
