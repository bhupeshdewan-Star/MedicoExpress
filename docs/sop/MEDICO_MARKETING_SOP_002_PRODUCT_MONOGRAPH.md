## 1. PURPOSE

To create scientifically accurate, compliant, evidence-based product monographs for HCP education and medical information purposes, adhering to UCPMP 2024, ABPI 2024, and FDA guidance on promotional materials.

---

## 2. SCOPE

Applies to:
- HCP-facing monographs (doctors, specialists, nurses)
- Patient education monographs (plain language)
- Medical information resource documents
- Scientific briefing documents for advisory boards

---

## 3. QUALITY GATES (Pre-Release Mandatory)

1. **On-Label Verification** — All claims within approved indication, dosage, population
2. **Substantiation Check** — Every efficacy/safety claim backed by published evidence with citation
3. **Fair-Balance Assessment** — Benefits and risks discussed with proportional weight
4. **Regulatory Compliance** — No absolute safety language; no misleading superiority claims
5. **Promotional Language Review** — Free of exaggerated patient/HCP benefit language
6. **Medical-Legal-Regulatory Review** — Sign-off from Medical + Legal + Regulatory

## 5. CORE SECTIONS REQUIRED (HCP Monograph)

### 5.1 Clinical Overview
- **Purpose:** Concise summary of product positioning
- **Required Elements:**
  - INN and trade name
  - FDA approval status and indication
  - Primary mechanism of action (layman's terms)
  - Key clinical efficacy (1-2 sentences)
  - Safety profile summary
  - Recommended starting dose
- **Substantiation:** FDA label, published efficacy/safety summaries
- **Word Count:** 100-150 words

### 5.2 Mechanism of Action (MOA)
- **Purpose:** Explain how drug works
- **Required Elements:**
  - Target (enzyme, receptor, pathway)
  - How does target achieve therapeutic effect
  - Comparison to other drugs in class (if applicable)
  - Why is this MOA important for the indication
- **Substantiation:** Published pharmacology literature, FDA label
- **Word Count:** 200-300 words
- **Critical Gate:** Must be scientifically accurate; no speculation

### 5.3 Indication & Patient Population
- **Purpose:** Define WHO can benefit
- **Required Elements:**
  - Full FDA-approved indication (verbatim from label)
  - Important limitations and contraindications
  - Special populations (elderly, renal/hepatic impairment, pregnancy)
  - Dose adjustments needed (by population)
- **Substantiation:** FDA label, clinical studies (Phase 3)
- **Word Count:** 200-250 words
- **Critical Gate:** Exactly on-label; no off-label suggestions

### 5.4 Efficacy Summary
- **Purpose:** Present clinical trial outcomes
- **Required Elements:**
  - Primary efficacy endpoint(s) and results
  - Secondary endpoints
  - Effect size and clinical significance
  - Comparison to standard of care (if published)
  - Onset of action and time to peak effect
  - Patient populations studied (demographics, disease severity)
- **Substantiation:** Published Phase 3 trials, FDA submission documents
- **Word Count:** 400-500 words
- **Critical Gate:** All claims substantiated; confidence intervals/p-values included

### 5.5 Safety Profile
- **Purpose:** Transparent communication of adverse events
- **Required Elements:**
  - Most common AEs (incidence >5%)
  - Serious AEs of interest (cardiac, hepatic, renal)
  - Safety in special populations
  - Drug-drug interactions (major, moderate)
  - Monitoring recommendations
  - Post-marketing safety data (if available)
- **Substantiation:** Clinical trial safety data, FDA label, post-marketing surveillance
- **Word Count:** 400-500 words
- **Critical Gate:** Fair balance maintained; risks not minimized

### 5.6 Dosage & Administration
- **Purpose:** Clear prescribing instructions
- **Required Elements:**
  - Recommended starting dose
  - Dose escalation (if any)
  - Maximum recommended dose
  - Dosing frequency
  - Instructions for special populations
  - Food/fasting requirements
  - Renal/hepatic dose adjustments
- **Substantiation:** FDA label
- **Word Count:** 150-200 words
- **Critical Gate:** Verbatim from approved label

### 5.7 Pharmacokinetics & Metabolism
- **Purpose:** Help HCP understand drug behavior
- **Required Elements:**
  - Absorption characteristics (bioavailability, food effects)
  - Half-life and implications for dosing
  - Metabolism pathway (liver, kidney)
  - Excretion route
  - Drug-drug interaction potential (major enzymes/transporters)
- **Substantiation:** Published PK studies, FDA label
- **Word Count:** 200-300 words

### 5.8 Comparative Efficacy (if applicable)
- **Purpose:** Position vs. standard of care
- **Required Elements:**
  - Head-to-head trial data (if published)
  - Indirect comparisons (meta-analyses)
  - Efficacy vs. dose/duration of therapy
  - Important differences (safety, convenience, cost)
  - Limitations of comparative data
- **Substantiation:** Published trials only; no speculative claims
- **Word Count:** 250-350 words
- **Critical Gate:** Comparative claims must be evidence-based; no unsupported superiority claims

### 5.9 Contraindications & Warnings
- **Purpose:** Identify at-risk populations
- **Required Elements:**
  - Absolute contraindications
  - Relative contraindications (use with caution)
  - Black box warnings (if any)
  - Precautions and monitoring recommendations
  - Use in pregnancy/lactation
- **Substantiation:** FDA label
- **Word Count:** 200-250 words
- **Critical Gate:** Verbatim from label; no minimization

### 5.10 Clinical Guidance & Case Studies
- **Purpose:** Illustrate real-world use
- **Required Elements:**
  - 2-3 realistic patient case studies (anonymized)
  - Decision tree for patient selection
  - When to switch therapy
  - When to combine with other agents
  - Management of common side effects
- **Substantiation:** Published case reports, expert consensus (clearly labeled)
- **Word Count:** 300-400 words
- **Critical Gate:** Case studies realistic and representative; not cherry-picked

### 5.11 Managed Care & Reimbursement Information
- **Purpose:** Help HCP understand access
- **Required Elements:**
  - Reimbursement status (Medicare, commercial insurance, Medicaid)
  - Prior authorization requirements
  - Patient assistance programs
  - Copay assistance programs
  - Coverage restrictions (if any)
- **Substantiation:** Payer data, company program information
- **Word Count:** 150-200 words

### 5.12 Key References
- **Required Elements:**
  - All claims substantiated by citation
  - Format: Last Author Initials, Journal Abbreviation, Year;Volume(Issue):Pages
  - Include FDA label citation, key published trials, meta-analyses
  - Minimum 15-25 references
- **Critical Gate:** All references verified as published; no unpublished data

## 6. PROCEDURE: TWO-PASS DELIVERY

### PASS 1: DRAFT MONOGRAPH (Builder Agent)

**Step 1:** Gather source materials
- FDA label (approved indication, dosage, safety data)
- Phase 3 trial publications (primary efficacy/safety)
- Published comparative trials (if available)
- Post-marketing surveillance data (if available)
- Company clinical summary

**Step 2:** Draft all 12 sections
- Use Skill-Pharma-Product-Monograph for structural guidance
- Ensure on-label positioning throughout
- Cite all claims to source
- Draft recommendation: Go/Maybe/No-Go

**Step 3:** First pass output
- Complete 12-section monograph
- Each section has claims with [bracketed evidence markers]
- Red flag any sections needing clarification

### PASS 2: QA + COMPLIANCE REVIEW

**Step 2a: QA Review (QA Agent)**
- ✅ All 12 sections present
- ✅ Word counts appropriate per section
- ✅ Claims substantiated with citations
- ✅ Tone professional and scientific
- ✅ Structure clear and logical

**Step 2b: Compliance Review (Compliance Agent)**
- 🔐 **On-Label Check:** Skill-On-Label-Validator
  - No off-label indications suggested
  - No off-label dosages or populations
  - Limitations and contraindications clearly stated
  
- 🔐 **Fair-Balance Check:** Skill-Fair-Balance-Assessor
  - Benefits not exaggerated
  - Risks not minimized
  - Safety data proportional to efficacy data
  - No absolute safety language ("completely safe")
  
- 🔐 **Promotional Risk Check:** Skill-Promotional-Language-Validator
  - No misleading superiority claims
  - No emotional language or patient testimonials
  - Language neutral and factual
  - Comparisons to competitors evidence-based

**Step 2c: Rework (Builder Agent)**
- If QA/Compliance flags issues, Builder revises
- Maximum 2 revision cycles before escalation

### PASS 3: FINAL APPROVAL

**Step 3:** Medical-Legal-Regulatory (MLR) Sign-Off
- **Medical Officer:** Confirms scientific accuracy
- **Legal Officer:** Confirms no legal exposure
- **Regulatory Officer:** Confirms compliance with UCPMP/ABPI/FDA guidance
- All three signatures required for release
