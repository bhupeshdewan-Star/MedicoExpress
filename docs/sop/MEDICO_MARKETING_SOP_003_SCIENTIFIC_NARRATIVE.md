## 1. PURPOSE

To develop compelling, evidence-based scientific narratives that position a pharmaceutical product within the therapeutic landscape. These narratives are foundational to all marketing communications and ensure consistent, compliant messaging.

---

## 2. SCOPE

Applies to:
- Product launch campaign narratives
- Therapeutic area positioning statements
- Medical congress presentations
- Scientific advisory board materials
- Medical representative training materials
- HCP educational content

---

## 3. DEFINITION

A **Scientific Narrative** is a cohesive, data-driven story that explains:
1. **The Medical Need** — Why is this disease/condition important?
2. **The Problem with Current Care** — What are limitations of standard of care?
3. **The Solution** — How does this product address the unmet need?
4. **The Evidence** — What clinical data supports this positioning?
5. **The Implications** — How does this change clinical practice?

---

## 4. CORE NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### Part 1: Disease Landscape (500-700 words)
- **Incidence/Prevalence:** How many patients affected (global, US, key geographies)
- **Disease Burden:** Mortality, morbidity, QOL impact, economic burden
- **Patient Journey:** Typical symptom onset → diagnosis → treatment path
- **Key Opinion Leaders:** Who are recognized experts in this space?
- **Unmet Medical Needs:** What gaps exist in current care?

### Part 2: Current Standard of Care (400-600 words)
- **Existing Therapies:** What drugs/interventions are first-, second-, third-line?
- **Efficacy Limitations:** What are success rates? How many patients fail?
- **Safety Concerns:** Common adverse events, contraindications, monitoring burden
- **Convenience Issues:** Dosing frequency, administration route, patient adherence
- **Limitations of Approach:** Why isn't current care adequate?

### Part 3: Product Mechanism & Rationale (400-500 words)
- **Pharmacological Innovation:** Why is this new MOA important?
- **Expected Clinical Benefit:** How does mechanism translate to patient benefit?
- **Differentiation from Competitors:** How is this different/better?
- **Theoretical Advantages:** What can we expect vs. current standard?

### Part 4: Clinical Evidence Summary (800-1000 words)
- **Phase 2 Data:** Proof of concept, dose selection, safety signals
- **Phase 3 Data:** Primary efficacy endpoint, effect size, statistical significance
- **Secondary Endpoints:** Additional clinical benefits shown
- **Safety Profile:** Incidence of common/serious AEs, safety signal absence
- **Special Populations:** Data in elderly, renal impairment, hepatic impairment, pregnancy
- **Comparative Data:** How does it compare to standard of care? (head-to-head vs. indirect)

### Part 5: Clinical Implications & Practice Change (500-600 words)
- **Positioning in Therapeutic Landscape:** Where does this fit?
- **Patient Selection:** Which patients benefit most?
- **Treatment Algorithm:** When to initiate, escalate, combine?
- **Expected Practice Impact:** How should HCP treatment approach change?
- **Key Uncertainties:** What questions remain? What post-marketing studies planned?

### Part 6: Market Opportunity (200-300 words)
- **Target Patient Population:** How many can be treated?
- **Reimbursement Status:** Coverage, prior authorization, copay implications
- **Commercial Positioning:** Price point, value proposition
- **Geographic Priorities:** Launch sequence, expansion plans

---

## 5. QUALITY GATES

1. **Narrative Coherence** → Story flows logically, each section builds on prior
2. **Evidence Base** → Every fact/statistic substantiated by published source
3. **On-Label Positioning** → Claims consistent with approved indication/label
4. **Fair-Balance** → Benefits and risks proportionally weighted
5. **Regulatory Compliance** → UCPMP/ABPI/FDA-compliant language
6. **Audience Appropriateness** → Tone/complexity matched to HCP audience

---

## 6. PROCEDURE: TWO-PASS DELIVERY

### PASS 1: DRAFT NARRATIVE (Builder Agent)
- Gather disease epidemiology, current therapy literature, product clinical data
- Draft all 6 sections with claims tied to published sources
- Ensure logical flow and compelling storyline
- Flag any sections with insufficient evidence

### PASS 2: REVIEW & REFINEMENT
- **QA Agent:** Verify completeness, citations, coherence
- **Compliance Agent:** Verify on-label positioning, fair-balance, regulatory compliance
- **Therapeutic Area Expert:** Validate clinical accuracy and landscape assessment
- **Revision:** Up to 2 cycles before escalation

---

## 7. ESCALATION RULES

| Issue | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Insufficient clinical data to support claim | Source Resolver | 24h |
| Off-label positioning | Compliance Officer | 24h |
| Competitive claim without evidence | Legal Officer | 48h |
| Expert disagreement on medical need | CMO + Therapeutic Area Expert | 48h |
