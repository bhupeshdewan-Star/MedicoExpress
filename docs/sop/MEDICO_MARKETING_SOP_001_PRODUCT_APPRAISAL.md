## 1. PURPOSE

To systematically evaluate pharmaceutical products across 16 weighted dimensions to generate a scored appraisal for in-licensing, acquisition, internal development continuation, or portfolio decisions. This SOP ensures consistent, evidence-based product evaluation across the organization.

---

## 2. SCOPE

Applies to:
- Pre-clinical candidates under consideration for in-licensing
- Clinical-stage candidates for acquisition evaluation
- Marketed products under portfolio expansion review
- Generic/biosimilar opportunities
- Line extension candidates

**Out of Scope:** Existing internal compounds already approved for development (use separate Portfolio Management SOP)

---

## 3. DEFINITIONS

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **INN** | International Nonproprietary Name (generic name) |
| **Therapeutic Area** | Medical specialty focus (e.g., Gastroenterology, Oncology) |
| **Stage** | Development stage (Discovery, Preclinical, IND-enabling, Phase 1-3, NDA/MAA, Approved, Launched) |
| **Red Flag** | Critical issue that requires escalation (regulatory hold, safety signal, IP gap) |
| **Appraisal Score** | Composite 0-100 scale based on 16 weighted categories |
| **Substantiation** | Evidence source (published study, regulatory filing, clinical data) |

---

## 4. GOVERNING REGULATIONS & STANDARDS

- **ICH Guidelines** (GCP, CTD)
- **FDA Guidance** (Drug Approval Process, Patent Strategies)

## 6. PROCEDURE

### 6.1 INTAKE & SCOPE LOCK

**Step 1:** Receive product appraisal request with:
- Product name (trade + INN)
- Current development stage
- Therapeutic area
- Target geographies
- Appraisal objective (in-license, acquisition, internal dev continuation, portfolio expansion)
- Decision deadline

**Step 2:** Verify data availability
- Gather: Clinical summary, regulatory pathway, IP landscape, competitive analysis, target indication(s)
- Escalate if critical data missing (e.g., Phase 2 efficacy data for primary indication)

**Step 3:** Document scope in Project Memory
- Affected product, timeline, decision authority
- SOPs/skills that apply (this SOP-MM-001 + Skill-Pharma-Product-Appraisal)
- Risk level (high = regulatory uncertainty; medium = competitive risk; low = routine)

---

### 6.2 SCIENTIFIC MERIT ASSESSMENT (Weight: 20%)

**Evaluate:**
1. **Mechanism of Action (MOA)** (0-10 points)
   - Is MOA novel vs. class? Is it well-characterized?
   - Evidence: Published literature, IND/CTA application, company data
   
2. **Efficacy Profile** (0-10 points)
   - Does product show meaningful clinical benefit in primary indication?
   - Compare to standard of care (effect size, response rate, symptom improvement)
   - Evidence: Phase 2/3 trials, published abstracts, regulatory submissions

3. **Safety Profile** (0-10 points)
   - What is adverse event profile? Are there safety signals or concerns?
   - Expected vs. observed AEs; any signals in post-marketing surveillance
   - Evidence: IND safety updates, clinical trial data, published literature

**Scoring Guidance:**
- 9-10: Compelling efficacy, best-in-class or first-in-class, minimal safety concerns
- 7-8: Clear efficacy over SOC, acceptable safety profile
- 5-6: Modest efficacy, manageable safety; differentiation unclear
- 3-4: Limited efficacy or significant safety concerns
- 1-2: Efficacy not demonstrated or serious safety issues
- 0: Unacceptable efficacy or safety; recommend rejection

**Red Flags to Escalate:**
- Unexplained safety signal or FDA safety communication
- Efficacy fails to meet pre-specified primary endpoint
- Mechanism not adequately characterized

---

### 6.3 REGULATORY PATHWAY ASSESSMENT (Weight: 15%)

**Evaluate:**
1. **Regulatory Status** (0-10 points)
   - How advanced is the regulatory filing? Are there orphan designations, breakthrough designations?
   - Timeline to approval in target geographies
   - Evidence: Regulatory correspondence, FDA/EMA interaction records

2. **Regulatory Risk** (0-10 points)
   - Are there outstanding deficiencies, CRL comments, or unresolved issues?
   - What is probability of approval in next 2 years?
   - Evidence: Regulatory agency communications, approval timelines

**Scoring Guidance:**
- 9-10: NDA/MAA approved or clear pathway with minimal risk
- 7-8: Phase 3 ongoing with positive interim data; clear approval path
- 5-6: Phase 2 complete; regulatory pathway defined but some uncertainty
- 3-4: Phase 2 ongoing or Phase 3 failures requiring additional studies
- 1-2: Significant regulatory hurdles or CRL with major deficiencies
- 0: Regulatory rejection or abandoned program

**Red Flags:**
- Outstanding CRL (Complete Response Letter) with unresolved deficiencies
- Regulatory hold or clinical hold
- Indication narrower than anticipated

---

### 6.4 INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP) ASSESSMENT (Weight: 10%)

**Evaluate:**
1. **Patent Protection** (0-10 points)
   - Are there composition patents, method-of-use patents, or formulation patents?
   - Estimated patent life (years of exclusivity remaining)
   - Any freedom-to-operate concerns?
   - Evidence: Patent databases (Google Patents, WIPO), company IP records

2. **Exclusivity Strategy** (0-10 points)
   - Are there opportunities for orphan designation, patent term extension, data exclusivity?
   - What is competitive IP landscape for same indication?
   - Evidence: Patent landscape analysis, regulatory guidance

**Scoring Guidance:**
- 9-10: Strong patent position with 10+ years of protection
- 7-8: Patent protection 5-10 years; some exclusivity opportunities
- 5-6: Patent protection 2-5 years; limited exclusivity strategies
- 3-4: Patent protection < 2 years; significant generic competition risk
- 1-2: Patents weak or challenged; high generic risk
- 0: No patent protection; patent already expired

**Red Flags:**
- Freedom-to-operate issue (infringe on third-party patents)
- Patent challenge or litigation
- Patent expiration imminent before product launch

---

### 6.5 CLINICAL EVIDENCE QUALITY (Weight: 10%)

**Evaluate:**
1. **Study Quality** (0-10 points)
   - Were trials well-designed (randomized, blinded, adequate sample size)?
   - Were primary endpoints met? Were secondary endpoints supportive?
   - Evidence: Clinical study reports, published papers, regulatory agency reviews

2. **Patient Population** (0-10 points)
   - Is the studied population relevant to target market?
   - Are inclusion/exclusion criteria reasonable?
   - Evidence: Protocol review, demographics analysis

**Scoring Guidance:**
- 9-10: Phase 3 trials well-designed, primary + multiple secondary endpoints met, representative population
- 7-8: Phase 3 trials adequate quality, primary endpoint met, acceptable population
- 5-6: Phase 2 trials showing promise or Phase 3 with moderate effect size
- 3-4: Limited clinical data or modest efficacy signal
- 1-2: Weak clinical evidence or trial design concerns
- 0: Insufficient clinical data to assess

---

### 6.6 PHARMACOKINETIC/PHARMACODYNAMIC (PK/PD) PROFILE (Weight: 5%)

**Evaluate:**
- Absorption characteristics (oral bioavailability, food effects)
- Half-life and dosing frequency implications
- Metabolism (liver/kidney involvement, drug-drug interactions)
- Dose proportionality and therapeutic drug monitoring

**Scoring:** 0-10 (favorable PK/PD = 9-10; complex PK/PD = 5-6; problematic = 1-2)

**Red Flags:**
- Narrow therapeutic window
- Significant drug-drug interactions
- Metabolism via polymorphic enzymes affecting efficacy

---

### 6.7 MANUFACTURING & SUPPLY CHAIN (Weight: 5%)

**Evaluate:**
- CMC (Chemistry, Manufacturing, Controls) status
- Supply chain stability and manufacturing complexity
- GMP compliance and inspection history
- Cost of goods estimates

**Scoring:** 0-10 (established manufacturing = 9-10; developing = 5-6; problematic = 1-2)

**Red Flags:**
- GMP deficiencies or inspection findings
- Single-source supplier with no backup
- Complex chemistry limiting manufacturability

---

### 6.8 MARKET OPPORTUNITY & COMMERCIAL POTENTIAL (Weight: 10%)

**Evaluate:**
1. **Market Size** (0-10 points)
   - What is target indication prevalence?
   - Expected market size (units/revenue) in 5 years?
   - Evidence: Market research, epidemiology literature, sales forecasts

2. **Competitive Landscape** (0-10 points)
   - Who are competitors? What are their market shares?
   - Is there market room for new product?
   - How differentiated is this product vs. competitors?
   - Evidence: Competitive intelligence, market share data

**Scoring Guidance:**
- 9-10: Large market (>$1B potential), minimal competition, clear differentiation
- 7-8: Moderate market ($100M-1B), limited competition, some differentiation
- 5-6: Niche market ($10-100M), several competitors, modest differentiation
- 3-4: Small market (<$10M) or highly competitive
- 1-2: Limited market opportunity or commoditized
- 0: No viable market

**Red Flags:**
- Market shrinking (newer agents replacing standard of care)
- Product not differentiated from existing alternatives
- Reimbursement unlikely or restricted

---

### 6.9 PRICING & REIMBURSEMENT STRATEGY (Weight: 5%)

**Evaluate:**
- Expected pricing vs. standard of care
- Reimbursement landscape (government, private insurance, patient assistance)
- Health economics value proposition
- Regulatory pricing constraints (reference pricing, price caps)

**Scoring:** 0-10 (favorable pricing/reimbursement = 9-10; uncertain = 5-6; limited = 1-2)

**Red Flags:**
- Restricted reimbursement or prior authorization requirements
- Price cap regulations limiting profitability
- Value proposition not supported by health economics data

---

### 6.10 STRATEGIC FIT & PORTFOLIO ALIGNMENT (Weight: 5%)

**Evaluate:**
- Alignment with company strategic priorities
- Synergies with existing portfolio (sales force, infrastructure, patient populations)
- Therapeutic area focus alignment
- Geographic priority alignment

**Scoring:** 0-10 (excellent fit = 9-10; moderate fit = 5-6; poor fit = 1-2)

---

### 6.11 TWO-PASS QUALITY ASSURANCE

**Pass 1: Initial Appraisal (Medical Evaluator)**
- Complete all 16 categories
- Identify red flags
- Draft summary recommendation (Go/Maybe/No-Go)

**Pass 2: QA & Expert Review (Therapeutic Area Expert + Regulatory Advisor)**
- Validate scoring accuracy
- Review substantiation (sources)
- Confirm red flag assessment
- Validate recommendation

**Revision Loop:**
- If QA finds errors or gaps, return to Medical Evaluator for correction
- Maximum 2 revision cycles before escalation to Compliance Officer

---

### 6.12 COMPLIANCE & APPROVAL GATE

**Quality Gates to Pass:**
1. ✅ **Source Traceability** — Every claim has supporting evidence (publication, regulatory filing, company data)
2. ✅ **Completeness** — All 16 categories scored; red flags documented
3. ✅ **Conflict of Interest** — No COI disclosures for evaluators
4. ✅ **Regulatory Alignment** — Recommendations comply with company policies
5. ✅ **Accuracy** — Scoring internally consistent and defensible

**Approval Authority:** Compliance Officer signs off; Appraisal now releasable

|---------------|-------------|----------|--------|

| Regulatory Hold | VP Regulatory Affairs | 24 hours | Determine approvability |

## 10. DECISION DOCUMENTATION & MEMORY UPDATE

**After Approval:**
1. Log decision in project memory (product name, score, recommendation, date, approver)
2. Store final appraisal in controlled repository
3. Update skill trainer with lessons learned (e.g., "scoring pattern for similar products")
4. Archive all supporting evidence

**Quarterly Review:**
- Are appraisal scores predictive of actual product success?
- Are there blind spots in the 16-dimension framework?
- Recommend SOP updates based on lessons learned
