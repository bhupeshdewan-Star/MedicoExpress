## 1. PURPOSE

To develop scientifically accurate, visually compelling slide decks for HCP presentations at case-based roundtables, medical congresses, advisory boards, and training events, ensuring compliance with promotional guidelines.

---

## 2. SCOPE

Applies to:
- Case-based roundtable presentations
- Medical congress presentations
- Advisory board materials
- HCP training presentations
- Scientific symposia presentations
- Webinar slides for healthcare professionals

---

## 3. QUALITY GATES

1. **Visual Clarity** → Slides easy to read; data/graphs clearly presented
2. **Evidence-Based** → All claims substantiated with cited sources
3. **On-Label** → Product claims consistent with approved indication
4. **Fair-Balance** → Benefits and risks discussed proportionally
5. **Regulatory Compliance** → UCPMP/ABPI/FDA-compliant language and claims
6. **Speaker Notes** → Detailed speaker notes provided for every slide
7. **Professional Design** → Adheres to company brand guidelines

---

## 4. SLIDE DECK STRUCTURE (20-30 slides)

### Section 1: Title & Agenda (2 slides)
- **Slide 1:** Title slide with speaker name, date, disclosures
- **Slide 2:** Agenda for presentation

### Section 2: Disease Landscape (3-4 slides)
- **Slide 3:** Disease epidemiology (incidence, prevalence)
- **Slide 4:** Disease burden (mortality, QOL impact)
- **Slide 5:** Unmet medical needs
- **Slide 6** (optional): Patient journey/natural history

### Section 3: Current Standard of Care (2-3 slides)
- **Slide 7:** Existing therapy options
- **Slide 8:** Efficacy/safety of current standard
- **Slide 9:** Limitations of current care (gaps, side effects, convenience)

### Section 4: Product Overview (3-4 slides)
- **Slide 10:** Product mechanism of action
- **Slide 11:** Why this mechanism matters
- **Slide 12:** Product positioning vs. competitors
- **Slide 13** (optional): Dosage/administration

### Section 5: Clinical Evidence (5-7 slides)
- **Slide 14:** Phase 2 proof of concept
- **Slide 15:** Phase 3 primary endpoint results
- **Slide 16:** Secondary endpoints
- **Slide 17:** Safety profile (adverse event summary)
- **Slide 18:** Comparative efficacy (head-to-head or indirect comparison)
- **Slide 19:** Special populations (elderly, renal/hepatic impairment)
- **Slide 20:** Practice-changing data or key takeaway

### Section 6: Clinical Implications (2-3 slides)
- **Slide 21:** Where does this fit in treatment algorithm?
- **Slide 22:** Patient selection criteria
- **Slide 23:** Expected clinical outcomes

### Section 7: Practical Considerations (2-3 slides)
- **Slide 24:** Dosage/administration convenience
- **Slide 25:** Reimbursement/access information
- **Slide 26:** Drug-drug interactions/monitoring

### Section 8: Case Study (2-3 slides)
- **Slide 27-29:** Real-world case study (anonymized)
  - Patient presentation
  - Treatment approach
  - Outcomes
  - Key learnings

### Section 9: Conclusions & Key Takeaways (1-2 slides)
- **Slide 30:** Summary of key points
- **Slide 31** (optional): Q&A discussion points

### Section 10: Disclosures & References (2 slides)
- **Slide 32:** Speaker disclosures
- **Slide 33:** References and further reading

---

## 5. SLIDE DESIGN STANDARDS

- **Visual Elements:** High-quality graphs, charts, images (all labeled, sourced)
- **Text:** Bullet points only; no paragraph blocks
- **Color:** Brand-compliant; high contrast for readability
- **Data Visualization:** Clear labeling of axes, legends, units; error bars where appropriate
- **Citations:** Small font citation on each evidence-based slide (e.g., "Smith et al. JAMA 2024")

---

## 6. SPEAKER NOTES REQUIREMENT

Every slide must include detailed speaker notes (100-200 words) containing:
- Key message for this slide
- Supporting evidence/statistics
- Anticipated questions and answers
- Transition to next slide

---

## 7. PROCEDURE: TWO-PASS DELIVERY

### PASS 1: DRAFT DECK (Builder Agent)
- Research clinical evidence, epidemiology, competitive landscape
- Draft all slides with placeholder graphics and citations
- Write detailed speaker notes for every slide
- Create speaker presentation guide

### PASS 2: REVIEW & REFINEMENT
- **QA Agent:** Verify slide completeness, text clarity, graph accuracy
- **Compliance Agent:** Verify all claims substantiated, on-label, fair-balance
- **Design Specialist:** Verify brand compliance, visual clarity, professional appearance
- **Medical Expert:** Verify scientific accuracy and appropriateness for audience
- **Revision:** Up to 2 cycles before escalation

---

## 8. QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLIST

- ✅ All 30+ slides present and complete
- ✅ All graphs/data labeled and cited
- ✅ Speaker notes present for every slide
- ✅ No off-label claims
- ✅ Benefits and risks balanced
- ✅ No absolute safety language
- ✅ No unsupported comparative claims
- ✅ Disclosures present and accurate
- ✅ Brand-compliant design
- ✅ Regulatory officer sign-off obtained

---

## 9. ESCALATION RULES

| Issue | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Unsupported comparative claim | Legal Officer | 24h |
| Off-label suggestion | Compliance Officer | 24h |
| Design non-compliance | Brand/Design Manager | 24h |
| Data presentation ambiguity | Medical Expert | 24h |

---

## 10. APPROVAL & RELEASE

- **Medical Officer Sign-off:** Scientific accuracy
- **Regulatory Officer Sign-off:** UCPMP/ABPI/FDA compliance
- **Compliance Officer Sign-off:** No legal/promotional risk
- **Brand Manager Sign-off:** Design compliance
- All four signatures required before release
