   - Symptom relief: 78% vs. 58% at week 4 (p<0.001)
   - H. pylori: 95% vs. 88% eradication (p=0.042), NNT=14
   - Why more effective: Better acid suppression, antibiotic bioavailability, H. pylori stress

5. **Part 5: Clinical Practice Impact** (600 words)
   - GERD management shift: Faster relief, new option for failures
   - H. pylori eradication: Higher success, fewer rescue therapies, cost savings
   - Patient populations: PPI failures (1.5-2M), B12 deficiency risk, CYP interactions, H. pylori

6. **Part 6: Market Opportunity** (400 words)
   - Addressable population: 2-3.5M/year
   - Peak sales: $100-150M/year
   - Competitive positioning: Only P-CAB option, clear differentiation
